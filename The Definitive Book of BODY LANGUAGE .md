## All Things Are Not What They Seem
*(Cheezein hamesha waise nahi hoti jaise dikhti hain)*

Kisi ke saath asal mein kya ho raha hai — yeh samajhna simple hai — easy nahi, lekin simple zaroor. Yeh sab uss maahol mein jo kuch tum dekhte aur sunte ho, usse milate hue aur theek nateeje nikalne ke baare mein hai. Lekin zyaadatar log sirf wahi dekhte hain jo unhe lagta hai ki woh dekh rahe hain. Is baat ko samjhaane ke liye ek kahaani:

---

Do aadmi jungle mein chal rahe the jab unhe ek bada gehra gaddha mila.

*"Waah… yeh toh kaafi gehra lagta hai,"* ek ne kaha. *"Chalo kuch kankar daalo andar, dekho kitna gehra hai."*

Unhone kuch kankar daale aur intezaar kiya — lekin koi awaaz nahi aayi.

*"Yaar — yeh sach mein bahut gehra gaddha hai. Chalo koi bada patthar daalo. Isse toh awaaz aayegi."*

Unhone do football size ke pathar uthaye aur gaddhe mein daale, phir intezaar kiya — phir bhi kuch nahi suna.

*"Wahan paas mein ek railway sleeper padi hai,"* ek ne kaha. *"Agar hum ise daalein, toh pakka koi awaaz aayegi."*

Unhone woh bhaari sleeper ghaseet ke gaddhe ke paas laaye aur andar pheka — lekin gaddhe se koi awaaz nahi aayi.

Tabhi, paas ke jungle se ek bakra aaya — hawaon ki tarah bhaagta hua. Woh seedha dono aadmiyon ki taraf aaya, unke beech se guzra, poori taqat se dauda, phir ek uchhal liya aur gaddhe mein samaа gaya. Dono aadmi hairan-pareshan (astonished) khadey reh gaye.

Jungle se ek kisan nikla — *"Hey! Kya tumne mera bakra dekha?"*

*"Haan pakka! Yeh sabse ajeeb cheez thi jo humne kabhi dekhi! Woh jungle se hawaon ki tarah bhaagta aaya aur us gaddhe mein kood gaya!"*

*"Nahi,"* kisan ne kaha. *"Woh mera bakra nahi ho sakta. Mera bakra toh ek railway sleeper se bandha hua tha!"*

## How Well Do You Know the Back of Your Hand?
*(Apni haath ki peeth ko tum kitna jaante ho?)*

Hum kabhi kabhi kehte hain ki hum kisi cheez ko "apni haath ki peeth ki tarah" jaante hain — lekin experiments prove karte hain ki 5 percent se bhi kam log apni haath ki peeth ko ek photo mein pehchaan sakte hain.

Ek simple experiment ke results — jo humne ek television program ke liye kiye — dikhate hain ki zyaadatar log generally body language signals padhne mein bhi acche nahi hote.

Humne ek lambe hotel lobby ke aakhir mein ek bada aaina lagaya — jisse yeh dhoka (illusion) milta tha ki hotel mein enter karte waqt ek lamba corridor hotel ke aar-paar jaata hai. Humne chhat se bade-bade paudhe lakaaye — zameen se paanch feet ki unchaai tak — taaki jab bhi koi lobby mein enter kare, lagta ki doosri taraf se bhi koi aur guest enter kar raha hai.

Woh "doosra guest" aasaani se pehchaana nahi ja sakta tha kyunki paudhe uska chehra chupa lete the, lekin body aur chaal-dhal saaf dikhai deti thi. Har guest ne us doosre "guest" ko 5 se 6 second dekha, phir reception desk ki taraf mud gaya.

Jab poocha gaya ki kya unhone us doosre "guest" ko pehchana — **85 percent mardaron ne kaha: nahi.** Zyaadatar mardon ne khud ko aaine mein nahi pehchana. Ek ne toh kaha: *"Matlab woh mota, bekar dikhne wala banda?"*

Iske ulat, **58 percent aurton ne kaha ki yeh ek aaina tha** aur **30 percent ne kaha ki woh doosra "guest" jaana-pehchaana (familiar) laga.**

> **Zyaadatar mard aur lagbhag aadhi auraten nahi jaantein ki woh gardan ke neeche se kaisi dikhti hain.**

## How Well Can You Spot Body-Language Contradictions?
*(Body Language ki ulti-seedhi baaton ko tum kitna pakad sakte ho?)*

Har jagah logon mein politicians ki body language ke baare mein ek gehri dilchaspi paida ho gayi hai — kyunki sabko pata hai ki politicians kabhi kabhi kisi aisi cheez mein believe karne ka natak karte hain jis par unhe aslan yakeen nahi hota, ya yeh zahir karne ki koshish karte hain ki woh koi aur hain.

Politicians apna zyaadatar waqt bachte hue, talte hue, ignore karte hue, natak karte hue, jhooth bolte hue, apne jazbaat aur feelings chhupate hue, aur dhoka dene wali chaalein (smoke screens) use karte hue bitaate hain — aur crowd mein khayal-o-khawabon ke doston ko haath hilaate hue.

Lekin hum andar se jaante hain ki woh aakhirkaar apni ek-doosre se ulti (contradictory) body language signals ki wajah se pakde jaayenge — isliye hum unhe gaur se dekhna pasand karte hain, is intezaar mein ki unhe "pakad lein."

> **Kaise pata chalega ki ek politician jhooth bol raha hai?**
> **Uske hont hilte hain.**

Ek aur television show ke liye, humne ek local tourist bureau ki madad se ek experiment kiya. Tourists bureau mein local sightseeing aur doosri tourist jagahon ki jaankari lene aate. Unhe ek counter par bheja jaata jahan woh ek tourism officer se baat karte — ek aadmi jiske sone jaisi (blond) baalon wala, moochhon wala, white shirt aur tie mein tha.

Kuch minute yatra ke plans discuss karne ke baad, woh aadmi kuch brochures lene ke liye counter ke neeche ghum gaya. Phir counter ke neeche se ek bilkul alag aadmi nikla — saaf chehre wala, kaale baalon wala, neeli shirt pahne — haath mein brochures liye. Usne wahi baat jaari rakhi jahan se pehle aadmi ne chodi thi.

Taajjub ki baat — **lagbhag aadhe tourists ko koi badlaav hi nazar nahi aaya!** Aur mardon ne aurton se do guna zyada dhokha khaaya — unhone na sirf body language ka badlaav miss kiya, balki ek bilkul naye insaan ka aana bhi notice nahi kiya!

Jab tak tumhare andar yeh kaabiliyat janmjaat (innate) na ho ya tumne body language padhna seekha na ho — chances hain ki tum iska zyaadatar hissa miss kar rahe ho.

> **Yeh book tumhe dikhayegi ki tum kya miss karte aa rahe ho.**

## How We Wrote This Book
*(Humne Yeh Book Kaise Likhi)*

Barbara aur maine **The Definitive Book of Body Language** likhne ke liye mere asli book *Body Language* ko apna base banaya. Humne na sirf us book ko kaafi expand kiya, balki nayi scientific fields jaise evolutionary biology aur evolutionary psychology ki research bhi shamil ki — saath hi **MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging)** jaisi technology ka bhi istemal kiya jo dikhati hai ki brain mein kya hota hai.

Humne ise iss tarah likha hai ki ise kisi bhi page se kholkar padha ja sake. Content mainly body ke movements, expressions, aur gestures tak rakha hai — kyunki yeh wahi cheezein hain jo tumhe seekhni chahiye taaki har aamnay-samnay ki mulaqat ka poora faayda utha sako.

**The Definitive Book of Body Language** tumhe apne khud ke nonverbal signals ke baare mein zyada aware karega, aur dikhayega ki unhe effectively kaise use karein aur woh reactions kaise hasil karein jo tum chahte ho.

Yeh book body language aur gestures ke har hisse ko alag karke simple shabdon mein jaanchti hai taaki yeh tumhare liye aasaan ho. Waise, koi bhi gesture akele nahi hota — woh doosron ke saath milkar hota hai — isliye humne ek saath yeh bhi koshish ki hai ki cheezein zyada seedhi-saadhi (oversimplified) na lag jaayein.

Hamesha kuch log hote hain jo haath uthaakar kehte hain ki body language ka study sirf ek aur tarika hai jisse doosron ko dabaane (exploit) ya apne kaaboo mein karne ke liye unke raaz ya soch padhi jaaye. Lekin hum yeh maante hain ki yeh book tumhe apne saathiyon ke saath zyada gehri samajh deti hai — taaki tum doosron ko gehraai se samajh sako, aur isliye khud ko bhi.

Jab hum kisi cheez ke kaam karne ka tarika samajhte hain, toh uske saath jeena aasaan ho jaata hai. Lekin anjaan pan aur samajh ki kami darr aur andha-vishwaas ko janm deti hai, aur humein doosron ke baare mein zyada aalochanatmak (critical) banati hai.

Ek bird-watcher pakshiyon ka adhyayan isliye nahi karta ki unhe maar ke trophy ki tarah rakh sake. Usi tarah, body language ki knowledge aur skills ka maqsad yeh hai ki doosre insaan ke saath har mulaqat ek exciting anubhav ban jaaye.

## Your Body-Language Dictionary
*(Tumhari Body-Language Dictionary)*

Asli book sirf salespeople, managers, negotiators, aur executives ke liye ek kaam ki kitaab thi — lekin yeh book tumhari zindagi ke kisi bhi pahlu ke liye use ki ja sakti hai: ghar par, date par, ya kaam par.

**The Definitive Book of Body Language** is field mein humare milaye hue (cumulative) 30 saal se zyada ke gyan aur kaam ka natija hai. Hum tumhe woh basic vocabulary dete hain jo tumhe attitudes aur emotions padhne ke liye chahiye.

Yeh book tumhe un sabse uljhane wale (puzzling) sawaalon ke jawab degi jo tumhare mann mein hamesha se the — ki aakhir log kuch khaas tarike se kyun pesh aate hain — aur yeh hamesha ke liye tumhara apna pesh aana bhi badal degi.

**Yeh book waisi hai jaise kamre ki light jalana — taaki dekh sako woh sab jo hamesha wahan tha. Ab tum exactly jaanoge ki cheezein kya hain, kahan hain, aur unke baare mein kya karna hai.**

Har koi kisi aisa insaan ko jaanta hai jo logon se bhari ek room mein ghus jaata hai aur kuch hi minuton mein woh logon ke beech ke rishton aur unki feelings ka sahi-sahi andaaza laga leta hai. Kisi insaan ke rawaiye (attitudes) aur soch ko unke behaviour se padhne ki kabiliyat woh asli communication system tha jo insaanon ne bolne wali bhasha se bahut pehle use kiya tha.

Radio ke ijaad se pehle, zyaadatar communication books, letters, aur akhbaaron ke zariye hoti thi — matlab yeh tha ki Abraham Lincoln jaise bure dikhne wale aur kamzor bolne wale politicians bhi safal ho sakte the agar woh kaafi lambe time tak dete rahe aur achha likhte rahe. Radio ke daur ne un logon ke liye raasta khola jinka bolne wali bhasha par achha command tha — jaise Winston Churchill, jo bahut achhay se bolta tha — lekin aaj ke zyaada visual daur mein woh utna achieve (haasil) karne mein shayad mushkil paata.

Aaj ke politicians samajhte hain ki politics image aur appearance ke baare mein hai. Zyaadatar bade politicians ke paas ab personal body-language consultants (salahkaar) hain jo unhe sachcha, khayaal rakhne wala, aur imaandar dikhne mein madad karte hain — khaaskar tab jab woh actually aisa nahi hote.

Yeh lagbhag na-maanane wali baat lagti hai ki hamaari evolution (vikaas) ke hazaron saalon baad bhi, body language ka actively kisi bade scale par sirf 1960s ke baad se hi adhyayan kiya gaya — aur zyaadatar public ko is cheez ka pata tabhi chala jab humaari book *Body Language* 1978 mein publish hui.

Phir bhi zyaadatar log maante hain ki speech (bolna) hamesha se hamara communication ka main zariya raha hai. Evolutionary terms mein dekha jaaye toh speech humari communication ki duniya mein haaal mein hi aayi hai — aur mostly facts aur data batane ke liye use hoti hai. Speech pehli baar lagbhag 20 lakh saal se 5 lakh saal pehle develop hui hogi, jis dauran humaara brain teen guna bada ho gaya.

Usse pehle, body language aur galey se nikalti awaazein hi emotions aur feelings batane ke main tarike the — aur aaj bhi aisa hi hai. Lekin kyunki hum logon ke bolne wale words par focus karte hain, hum mein se zyaadatar body language se largely anjaan (uninformed) hain — yeh jaanne ki toh baat hi chodo ki yeh humaari zindagi mein kitni zaruri hai.

## In the Beginning…
*(Shuruaat Mein…)*

Silent-movie actors jaise Charlie Chaplin body language skills ke parishkar (pioneers) the — kyunki screen par communicate karne ka yahi ek zariya tha. Har actor ki skill ko acchi ya buri is baat se naapa jaata tha ki woh audience ko gestures aur body signals se kitna communicate kar sakta tha.

Jab talking films popular ho gayi aur acting ke nonverbal pahluon par kam zor diya jaane laga, toh bahut saare silent-movie actors gumnami (obscurity) mein kho gaye — sirf wahi bache jo verbal aur nonverbal dono mein achhe the.

Academic study ki baat karein toh, shayad sabse zyaada prabhavshali (influential) twentieth century se pehle ka kaam Charles Darwin ka tha — **"The Expression of the Emotions in Man and Animals"** — jo 1872 mein publish hua. Lekin yeh mainly academics hi padhte the. Phir bhi, isne modern studies ko janam diya — facial expressions aur body language ki — aur Darwin ke kaafi ideas aur observations duniya bhar ke researchers ne sahi sabit kar diye.

Tab se lekar, researchers ne lagbhag **10 lakh nonverbal cues aur signals** note karke record kiye hain.

1950s mein body language ke parishkar (pioneer) researcher **Albert Mehrabian** ne paaya ki kisi message ka total impact kuch aisa hota hai:
- **7% verbal** — sirf words
- **38% vocal** — awaaz ka lehen-dehen (tone), utaar-chadhav (inflection), aur doosri awaazein
- **55% nonverbal** — body language

> *"Tumne jis tarah kaha — woh zyaada matter karta hai, na ki tumne kya kaha."*

Manav vigyanik (anthropologist) **Ray Birdwhistell** ne nonverbal communication ki asli study ki neev rakhi — jise usne **"kinesics"** — yaani shareerik harakton ka adhyayan — kaha. Birdwhistell ka andaaza tha ki ek average insaan roz sirf **10–11 minute hi words bolne mein bitaata hai** aur ek average sentence sirf **2.5 second** leta hai.

Birdwhistell ne yeh bhi andaaza lagaya ki hum lagbhag **2,50,000 facial expressions** bana aur pehchaan sakte hain.

Mehrabian ki tarah, usne bhi paaya ki face-to-face conversation mein verbal hissa 35% se kam hota hai aur 65% se zyaada communication nonverbally hota hai.

1970s aur 1980s mein hazaron recorded sales interviews aur vaartaon (negotiations) ke humaare analysis ne dikhaya ki business mulaqaton (encounters) mein body language ek negotiating table par **60–80% impact** karti hai — aur log kisi naye insaan ke baare mein apni **60–80% pehli raai 4 minute se kam** mein bana lete hain.

Studies yeh bhi dikhati hain ki phone par negotiate karte waqt, jiske paas zyaada mazboot argument hota hai woh aksar jeetta hai — lekin face-to-face mein aisa nahi hota — kyunki hum overall apne final faisale zyaada is par lete hain ki hum kya dekhte hain, na ki kya sunte hain.

## Why It's Not What You Say
*(Baat Sirf Alfaazon Ki Nahi Hoti)*

Sahi lagne wali baat chahe kuch bhi ho — jab hum logon se pehli baar milte hain toh hum jaldi se unki milansaari (friendliness), dabbdbe (dominance), aur ek sambhavit (potential) saathi ke roop mein unke baare mein faisale karne lagte hain — aur aankhein pehli jagah nahi hoti jahan hum dekhte hain.

Zyaadatar researchers ab maante hain ki words mool roop se (primarily) information dene ke liye hain, jabki body language aapsi rishton ke rawaiye (interpersonal attitudes) tay karne ke liye use hoti hai — aur kuch cases mein verbal messages ki jagah bhi le leti hai.

Misal ke taur par, ek aurat kisi mard ko ek aise nazar se dekh sakti hai jo "maar daalti ho" — aur bina muh khole woh ek bahut saaf message pehuncha (convey) kar deti hai.

Culture chahe koi bhi ho, words aur movements itni andaazepan (predictability) ke saath saath aate hain ki Birdwhistell yeh daawa (claim) karne wale pehle insaan the ki ek achhe se trained insaan ko sirf kisi ki awaaz sun ke pata chal jaana chahiye ki woh kaun sa movement kar raha hai. Birdwhistell ne toh sirf gestures dekh ke yeh bhi bata dena seekh liya tha ki koi insaan kaun si language bol raha hai.

Kaafi log insaanon ko biologically animals maanana mushkil paate hain. Hum primates ki ek prajati (species) hain — Homo sapiens — ek baalon wala ape jo do paanon par chalna seekh gaya aur jiske paas ek chalak, viksit (advanced) brain hai. Lekin kisi bhi doosri prajati ki tarah, hum bhi biological rules se controlled hain jo humaare actions, reactions, body language, aur gestures ko control karte hain. Dilchasp baat yeh hai ki insaani jaanwar ko aksar pata hi nahi hota ki uski mudraayein (postures), movements, aur gestures kuch alag kahani bayan kar rahe hain jabki uski awaaz kuch aur bol rahi hoti hai.

## How Body Language Reveals Emotions and Thoughts
*(Body Language Emotions Aur Soch Ko Kaise Zahir Karti Hai)*

Body language kisi insaan ki emotional condition ka baahri pratibin (reflection) hai. Har gesture ya movement uss emotion ki ek valuable key ho sakti hai jo woh insaan uss waqt feel kar raha hota hai.

Misal ke taur par:
- Jo mard apna wajan badhne ko lekar apne aap ke baare mein sharminda (self-conscious) ho, woh apni thodi ke neeche ki twacha ko khench sakta hai.
- Jo aurat apni jangho par extra kilos ko lekar sochti ho, woh apni dress ko neeche smooth kar sakti hai.
- Jo insaan dara hua ya bachaawe ki sthiti (defensive) mein ho, woh apne baazoo cross kar sakta hai ya pair cross kar sakta hai ya dono.
- Ek mard jo badi-breasted aurat se baat kar raha ho, woh jaankaar taur par (consciously) uski chhaati ko dekhne se bachega — jabki saath hi anjaane mein (unconsciously) haath se pakadne wale (groping) gestures kar raha hoga.

Body language padhne ki key yeh hai ki kisi insaan ki emotional condition ko tab samjha jaaye jab tum yeh sun rahe ho ki woh kya keh raha hai — aur yeh bhi note karo ki woh woh baat kis maahol (context) mein keh raha hai. Isse tum fact ko fiction se aur haqeeqat (reality) ko khayal (fantasy) se alag kar sakte ho.

Haal hi mein, humein bolne wale words ka ek junoon (obsession) tha. Lekin zyaadatar log body-language signals aur unke impact se hairan karne wali had tak anjaan (remarkably unaware) hain — bawajood is baat ke ki hum ab jaante hain ki kisi bhi face-to-face conversation ke zyaadatar messages body signals ke zariye hi zahir (revealed) hote hain.

Misal ke taur par, France ke President Chirac, USA ke President Ronald Reagan, aur Australia ke Prime Minister Bob Hawke — teeno apne dimaaag mein issues ki relative sizes batane ke liye haath istemal karte the.

Bob Hawke ne ek baar politicians ke liye pay increases ki dafaa (defence) mein corporate executive salaries se comparison kiya. Usne claim kiya ki executive salaries bahut zyaada badhi hain aur politicians ki increases relatively chhoti hain. Jab bhi usne politicians ki income ka zikr kiya, usne haath ek yard door rakhe. Lekin jab usne executive salaries ka zikr kiya, usne haath sirf ek foot door rakhe — yeh zahir karta tha ki woh andar se maanta tha ki politicians asliyat mein bahut zyaada achha deal pa rahe hain, jo woh maanneeke liye taiyaar nahi tha.

## Why Women Are More Perceptive
*(Auraten Zyaada Teez Nazar Kyun Hoti Hain)*

Jab hum kehte hain ki koi insaan logon ke baare mein "teez nazar (perceptive)" ya "chhathi indri wala (intuitive)" hai, toh hum anjaane mein unki doosre ki body language padhne aur in cues ko verbal signals se compare karne ki kabiliyat ka zikr kar rahe hote hain.

Doosre lafzon mein, jab hum kehte hain ki hame ek andar se feel (hunch/gut feeling) hoti hai ki kisine jhooth bola hai — matlab usually yahi hota hai ki unki body language aur unke bolne wale words aapas mein match nahi kar rahe.

Isi ko speakers "audience awareness" — yaani audience ko padhne ki kabiliyat — kehte hain.

Agar ek audience apni seats par peeche jhuk ke baithi ho, thodi neeche aur baazoo seene par cross kiye — toh ek teez nazar (perceptive) speaker ko andar se yeh feel hoga ki uski delivery kaam nahi kar rahi. Woh samjhega ki audience ko involve karne ke liye alag approach leni hogi. Iske ulat, ek aisa speaker jo teez nazar (perceptive) na ho, woh aankhon ke andhe ki tarah aage badhta rahega.

> **Teez nazar (perceptive) hona matlab hai kisi ke words aur unki body language ke beech ke virodh (contradictions) ko pakadna.**

Overall, auraten mardoon se kahin zyaada teez nazar hoti hain — aur isi se woh cheez janam leti hai jise commonly **"aurat ki chhathi indri (women's intuition)"** kehte hain. Auraton mein nonverbal signals pakadne aur samajhne ki janmjaat (innate) kabiliyat hoti hai, saath hi chhoti-chhoti details ko pehchanne ki accurate nazar bhi.

Isi wajah se koi husband apni wife se zyaada jhooth bol ke baach nahi sakta — aur iske ulat, zyaadatar auraten bina mard ke samjhe use ullu bana sakti hain.

**Harvard University** ke psychologists ki research ne dikhaya ki auraten mardoon se kitni zyaada body language ke signals ke baare mein chaukann (alert) hoti hain. Unhone ek mard aur aurat ke beech communication ki short films dikhaayin — bina awaaz ke — aur participants se poocha gaya ki woh couple ke expressions dekh ke kya ho raha hai yeh batayein.

Results:
- **Auraton ne 87% baar sahi situation read ki**
- **Mardoon ne sirf 42% sahi read kiya**

"Nurturing" occupations mein mard — jaise artistic types, acting, aur nursing — auraton jitna hi achha karte hain. Gay (homosexual) mard bhi achha score karte hain.

Yeh kabiliyat khaaskar un auraton mein zyaada dikhti hai jo bacchon ki parvarish kar chuki hain — kyunki pehle kuch saalon mein maa almost sirf nonverbal channel par nirbhar (rely) karti hai bachche se communicate karne ke liye. Yahi wajah hai ki auraten aksar mardoon se zyaada achhi vaartakaar (negotiators) hoti hain.

## What Brain Scans Show
*(Brain Scans Kya Dikhate Hain)*

Zyaadatar auraton ke paas itna brain organization hai ki woh duniya ke kisi bhi mard se behtar communicate kar sakti hain.

**MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging)** brain scans saaf dikhate hain ki auraton ke paas doosron ke behaviour ko jaanchne (evaluate) karne ke liye mardoon ke mukable zyaada capacity kyun hai:

- Auraton ke paas doosron ke behaviour ko evaluate karne ke liye **14–16 brain areas**
- Mardoon ke paas sirf **4–6 brain areas**

Yeh explain karta hai ki kaise ek aurat kisi dinner party mein jaakar jald hi wahan ke doosre couples ke rishton ka haal bata sakti hai — kiska jhagda hua, kaun kisse achha lagta hai, wagera.

Yeh yeh bhi explain karta hai ki aurat ke nazariye se mard zyaada nahi bolte — aur mard ke nazariye se auraten kabhi chup nahi rehtin!

Jaise humne *"Why Men Don't Listen and Women Can't Read Maps"* mein dikhaya — female brain ek saath kai kaam (multi-tracking) ke liye organized hai. Ek average aurat ek saath **2–4 unrelated topics** handle kar sakti hai:
- TV dekho
- Phone par baat karo
- Peeche se doosri baat bhi suno
- Saath mein coffee bhi piyo

Woh ek conversation mein kai alag topics par baat kar sakti hai aur **5 vocal tones** use karti hai subject change karne ya points emphasize karne ke liye. Durbhagya se, zyaadatar mard sirf **3 tones** hi pehchaan paate hain — nateeja yeh hota hai ki auraten communicate karne ki koshish karti hain toh mard aksar baat ka sar-pair (plot) khoo baithte hain.

Studies dikhate hain ki jo insaan face-to-face kisi doosre ke behaviour ke baare mein hard visual evidence par nirbhar (rely) karta hai, woh zyaada sahi faisale karta hai — kisi sirf andar se feel (gut feeling) par nirbhar karne wale se behtar. Woh evidence body language mein hai — aur jabab auraten yeh anjaane mein (subconsciously) karti hain, koi bhi jaankaar taur par (consciously) signals padhna seekh sakta hai.

## How Fortune-Tellers Know So Much
*(Bhavishtya Batane Wale Itna Kyun Jaante Hain)*

Agar tumne kabhi kisi bhavishtya-vaktaa (fortune-teller) ke paas jaaye ho — toh tum shayad hairan hokay aaye hoge ki unhone tumhare baare mein aisi baatein batayin jo koi nahi jaanta tha — toh kya yeh ESP hai?

Bhavishtya-vaktaaon ke dhandhe ki research dikhati hai ki woh ek technique use karte hain jise **"cold reading"** kehte hain — ek aisa shakhs jo tumse pehle kabhi nahi mila, uske baare mein **~80% accuracy** de sakta hai.

Yeh un logon ke liye jaadui (magical) lag sakta hai jo sadhaseedhe (naive) aur kamzor (vulnerable) hain — lekin yeh actually sirf ek process hai jo body-language signals ki careful observation, insaani fitrat (human nature) ki samajh, aur sambhavna ke aankdon (probability statistics) par based hai.

Ise maanosik shaktiyon ka dawa karne waale (psychics), tarot-card readers, jyotishi (astrologers), aur hath dekhne wale (palm readers) information "read" karne ke liye use karte hain. Kaafi "cold readers" khud bhi anjaane mein yeh karte hain — aur isliye woh bhi maanne lagte hain ki unke paas sach mein "psychic" abilities hain!

Theek yahi maahol ke saath milaakar — tarot cards, crystal ball, aur thodi baazigari (theatrical setting) — ek aisa stage set ho jaata hai jo yaqeen dilata hai ki kuch ajeeb-o-ghareeb taaqatein kaam kar rahi hain — yahan tak ki ek sakht shakkiiye (hardened skeptic) ko bhi.

Sab kuch is baat par aata hai ki reader kisi ke reactions ko decode kare — jo statements par aur pooche gaye sawaalon par — aur ek insaan ke appearance ke simple observation se gathered information ko.

Zyaadatar "psychics" auraten hain — kyunki jaisa pehle discuss hua, unke paas extra brain wiring hai jo unhe babies ke body signals aur doosron ki emotional condition padhne deti hai.

### Tumhare Liye Ek Psychic Reading!

> *Main yeh bhi feel karta hoon ki kabhi kabhi tum bahut friendly, social, aur khule dil ke (outgoing) hote ho, aur kabhi kabhi apne aap mein simat (withdrawn), apne aap mein rahne wale (reserved), aur saavdhaan (cautious) bhi hote ho.*
>
> *Tum apni soch se chalne (independent thinker) par garoor karte ho — lekin saath hi jaante ho ki bina saboot ke doosron ki baatein seedha nahi maanein. Tum badlaav aur tanawwu (variety) pasand karte ho, lekin agar aadat aur paabandiyon (restrictions) se kaaboo kiya jaaye toh bechain (restless) ho jaate ho.*

> *Baahir se tum anushasit (disciplined) aur controlled dikhte ho — andar se tum concerned aur worried rehte ho — aur kabhi kabhi sochte ho ki tumne sahi choice ki ya nahi."*

Main tumhe bhavishtya-vaktaa banne ki toh taakeed (encourage) nahi karoonga, lekin tum jald hi doosron ko utni hi shuddhi (accuracy) se read kar paaoge.

## Inborn, Genetic, or Learned Culturally?
*(Janmjaat, Genetic, Ya Culturally Seekha Gaya?)*

Jab tum apne baazoo seene par cross karte ho — toh left over right karte ho ya right over left? Zyaadatar log confidently nahi bata sakte ki woh kya karte hain — jab tak try na karein. Abhi karo aur phir position reverse karo. Ek taraf comfortable lagti hai aur doosri bilkul galat.

Evidence suggest karta hai ki yeh ek genetic gesture ho sakta hai jise badla nahi ja sakta.

> **10 mein se 7 log left arm ko right ke upar cross karte hain.**

Yeh discover karne ke liye bahut saari debate aur research hui hai ki nonverbal signals janmjaat (inborn) hain, seekhe gaye (learned) hain, genetic taur par transferred hain, ya kisi aur tarike se haasil (acquired) hue hain.

Evidence collect kiya gaya hai — andhe (blind) logon ke observations se, duniya bhar ke alag-alag cultures ke gestural behaviour se, aur humare nearest manav-vigyanik rishtedar (anthropological relatives) — apes aur monkeys ke behaviour ke adhyayan se.

Nateeje kuch aisa tha:

Zyaadatar primate babies suck karne ki kabiliyat ke saath paida hote hain — yeh janmjaat (inborn) ya genetic hai. German scientist **Eibl-Eibesfeldt** ne paaya ki bahra aur andha (deaf and blind) paida hue bacchon ki muskuraahaten (smiling expressions) bhi learning ya copying se alag aati hain — matlab yeh bhi janmjaat gestures hain.

**Ekman, Friesen, aur Sorenson** ne paanch widely different cultures ke logon ke facial expressions ka adhyayan kiya aur paaya ki har culture ne same basic facial gestures se emotion dikhaya — nateeja yeh nikla ki yeh gestures bhi janmjaat hain.

> **Sanskritik bhedabhaav (cultural differences) bahut saare hain, lekin basic body-language signals har jagah same hain.**

Debate abhi bhi jaari hai ki kuch gestures culturally seekhe jaate hain aur aadat (habitual) bante hain, ya genetic hain. Misal ke taur par:
- Zyaadatar **mard coat pehnte waqt pehle right arm daalte hain**
- Zyaadatar **auraten pehle left arm daaalti hain**

Yeh dikhata hai ki mard is action ke liye left brain hemisphere use karte hain jabki auraten right hemisphere use karti hain.

Jab ek mard bheed wali gali mein ek aurat ke paas se guzarta hai — toh woh usually uski taraf body turn karta hai. Aurat swabhaavt: (instinctively) apni body door kar leti hai apni chhati protect karne ke liye. Kya yeh ek janmjaat (inborn) female reaction hai, ya usne yeh anjaane mein (unconsciously) doosri auraton ko dekhkar seekha?

## Some Basic Origins
*(Kuch Buniyadi Shuruaatein)*

Zyaadatar basic communication signals duniya bhar mein same hain. Khush hone par log smile karte hain; dukhi ya gusse mein hone par maatthe par baal (frown) ya naak-bhaun chadhaana (scowl) karte hain.

**Sir hilana (haan mein)** lagbhag universally "yes" ya swikar (affirmation) ke liye use hota hai — yeh ek janmjaat gesture lagta hai kyunki andhe (blind) logon ke yahan bhi iska use hota hai.

**Sir ek taraf aur doosri taraf hilana (na mein)** nakaarne (negation) ke liye universal hai aur yeh shishukaal (infancy) mein seekha gaya gesture lagta hai. Jab ek baby ko poora milk pee chuka hota hai, toh woh apna sir side to side ghuma ke apni maa ki chhaati ko reject karta hai. Jab baccha khane se chhak jaata hai, toh woh apna sir side to side hila ke spoon-feeding rok deta hai — aur is tarah woh jaldi hi Head-Shaking gesture ko "na" ya naksaaratmak (negative) attitude dikhane ke liye use karna seekh jaata hai.

> **Head-Shaking gesture "no" ka signal deta hai — aur iska mool (origin) breastfeeding se aaya hai.**

Kuch gestures ka vikaasi mool (evolutionary origin) humaare aadim (primitive) animal past mein khoja ja sakta hai:

**Muskuraana (Smiling)** zyaadatar maansbhakshi (carnivorous) animals ke liye ek threat gesture hai — lekin primates ke liye yeh nonthreatening gestures ke saath milke samarpan (submission) dikhane ke liye hota hai.

**Daant dikhana aur naak phulana (Baring teeth and nostril flaring)** hamle (attacking) ke kaam se aaya hai — yeh aadim (primitive) signals hain jo doosre primates bhi use karte hain.

**Naak-bhaun chadhaana (Sneering)** janwaaron mein use hota hai doosoron ko khabardar (warn) karne ke liye ki zarurat parne par woh daant use kar sakte hain. Insaanon mein yeh gesture abhi bhi aata hai — halanki insaan aam tor par daanton se hamla nahi karte.

**Naak phulana (Nostril flaring)** sharir mein zyaada oxygen pahunchata hai — ladne ya bhaagne (fight or flight) ki tayyaari ke liye — aur primate duniya mein yeh doosron ko batata hai ki koi aasann khatraa (imminent threat) aane wala hai.

Insaani duniya mein, naak-bhaun chadhaana (sneering) gusse, chid (irritation), ya jab koi physical ya emotional khatre mein ho — ya kuch galat lage — tab aata hai.

## Universal Gestures
*(Har Jagah Istemal Hone Wale Gestures)*

**Kaandhe uchkaana (Shoulder Shrug)** ek universal gesture ka accha example hai jo tab use hota hai jab koi yeh nahi jaanta ya nahi samajhta ki tum kya keh rahe ho. Yeh ek multiple gesture hai jiske teen main hisse hain:

1. **Khulli hatheli (Exposed palms)** — yeh dikhane ke liye ki haath mein kuch chhupaaya nahi gaya
2. **Jhuke kaandhe (Hunched shoulders)** — gale ko hamle (attack) se bachane ke liye
3. **Ubhi hui bhaun (Raised brow)** — jo ek universal, samarpan wali (submissive) greeting hai

Jis tarah verbal language ek culture se doosri culture mein alag hoti hai — usi tarah kuch body-language signals bhi alag ho sakte hain. Ek gesture jo kisi ek culture mein common aur saaf ho sakta hai, woh kisi doosri culture mein bemaani (meaningless) ya bilkul alag meaning ka bhi ho sakta hai.

Sanskritik bhedabhaav (Cultural differences) baad mein Chapter 5 mein cover kiye jayenge.

## Three Rules for Accurate Reading
*(Sahi Reading Ke Liye Teen Niyam)*

Jo kuch bhi tum kisi situation mein dekhte aur sunte ho — woh zaruri nahi ki logon ke asli rawaiye (attitudes) ko zahir kare. Sahi padhne ke liye teen basic rules follow karne padenge.

---

### Rule 1: Read Gestures in Clusters
*(Gestures Ko Samuha Mein Padho)*

Body language seekhne wale naye (novice) logon ki sabse serious galtiyon mein se ek hai — ek akele gesture ko baaki gestures ya haalaat (circumstances) se alag karke vyakhya (interpret) karna.

Misal ke taur par, sar khujana kai cheezein mean kar sakta hai — paseena, ashhuddhi (uncertainty), dandruff, kide, bhulaakad, ya jhooth bolna — yeh depend karta hai ki usi waqt doosre kaun se gestures ho rahe hain.

Kisi bhi spoken language ki tarah, body language mein bhi words, sentences, aur punctuation hote hain. Har gesture ek single word ki tarah hai — aur ek word ke kai alag-alag meanings ho sakte hain. Jaise English mein "dressing" ke 10 se zyaada meanings hain — kapde pahenna, khane ka sauce, murge ka stuffing, ghav ki dressing, fertilizer, aur ghode ki grooming.

Kisi word ka poora matlab tab hi samajhta hai jab use sentence mein dusre words ke saath rakha jaaye. Gestures bhi "sentences" mein aate hain jinhein **"clusters"** kehte hain — aur yeh hamesha kisi insaan ki real feelings ya attitudes zahir karte hain.

Ek body-language cluster, bilkul verbal sentence ki tarah, sahi definition ke liye **kam se kam 3 "words"** chahiye hote hain.

> **Sar khujana ashhuddhi (uncertainty) mean kar sakta hai — lekin dandruff ki nishani bhi ho sakta hai.**

Isliye hamesha gesture clusters dekho sahi reading ke liye. Kai logon ki ek ya zyaada baar baar hone wali (repetitive) gestures hoti hain jo sirf yeh zahir karti hain ki woh bored hain ya pressure mein hain — jaise lagaataar baalon ko chhoona ya ghoomana — lekin akele mein, iska matlab aksar yeh hota hai ki insaan anxious (chhatapta hua) ya uncertain (ashuddha) hai.

---

**Critical Evaluation Cluster ka ek example:**

Jab koi sunne wala jo woh sun raha hai usse bepar'wah (unimpressed) ho, toh woh yeh gestures use karta hai:

- **Index finger cheek ki taraf point karti hai** — haath-se-chehra gesture
- **Ek aur ungli muh par** — as if rok raha ho
- **Angutha thodi support kar raha hai**
- **Pair tightly cross kiye** — bachaawe ki sthiti (defensive)
- **Baazoo body ke across** — bachaawe ki sthiti (defensive)
- **Sar aur thodi neeche** — naksaaratmak (negative)/virodhi (hostile)

Yeh body language "sentence" kuch aisa kehta hai: *"Mujhe jo tum keh rahe ho woh pasand nahi"* ya *"Main disagree karta hoon"* ya *"Main negative feelings rok raha hoon."*

---

### Rule 2: Look for Congruence
*(Mel Dhundho — Words Aur Body Match Karte Hain Ya Nahi)*

Research dikhati hai ki nonverbal signals verbal channel se **5 guna zyaada impact** karte hain — aur jab dono mel nahi khaate (incongruent) hote hain, toh log — khaaskar auraten — nonverbal message par nirbhar (rely) karti hain aur verbal content ko nazar-andaaz (disregard) kar deti hain.

Agar ek politician podium ke peeche khada ho, baazoo seene par tight fold kiye (bachaawe wali sthiti/defensive), thodi neeche (naksaaratmak/critical) — aur keh raha ho ki woh naujawanon ke ideas ke liye "khula aur willing" hai — toh kya tum convince ho sakte ho? Uske words aur gestures aapas mein mel nahi khaate (incongruent hain).

**Sigmund Freud** ne ek baar bataya tha ki jab ek patient verbally apni shaadi ki khushi bayan kar rahi thi — woh anjaane mein (unconsciously) apni wedding ring finger se nikaali aur pehna rahi thi. Freud ne is anjaane gesture (unconscious gesture) ki ehmiyat (significance) ko samjha aur surprise nahi hua jab baad mein shaadi ki problems saamne aayi.

> **Jab koi insaan ke words aur body language takraav (conflict) mein hon — auraten jo suna wo nazar-andaaz (ignore) kar deti hain.**

---

### Rule 3: Read Gestures in Context
*(Gestures Ko Maahol Mein Padho)*

Sabhi gestures uss maahol (context) mein consider kiye jaane chahiye jisme woh ho rahe hain.

Misal ke taur par, agar koi bus terminal par baith ke baazoo aur pair tight cross kiye hua ho, thodi neeche ho, aur bahar thand ho — toh iski wajah probably yeh hogi ki woh thanda feel kar raha hai, na ki woh bachaawe ki sthiti (defensive) mein hai.

Lekin agar woh same insaan wahi gestures kare jabki tum ek table par uske saamne baith ke use koi idea, product, ya service bech rahe ho — toh yeh sahi taur par vyakhya ki ja sakti hai ki woh naksaaratmak (negative) feel kar raha hai ya tumhara offer thukra (reject) raha hai.

## Why It Can Be Easy to Misread
*(Kyun Galat Padhna Aasaan Ho Sakta Hai)*

Kisi ka naram ya dheela haath milaana (soft or limp handshake) — khaaskar mard ka — aksar kamzor (weak) character ki nishaan maana jaata hai. Lekin agar kisi ke haath mein gathiya (arthritis) ho, toh woh mazboot handshake ki dard se bachne ke liye naram handshake use karega. Isi tarah, artists, musicians, surgeons, aur woh log jinka kaam naazuk (delicate) hai — jaise haath se kaam — aksar handshake nahi karna chahte, aur agar majboor ho jaayein toh "dead fish handshake" use kar sakte hain apne haath bachane ke liye.

Koi jo buri tarah fitting (ill-fitting) ya tight kapde pehnta ho, woh certain gestures use nahi kar sakta — aur yeh unki body language ko affect kar sakta hai. Misal ke taur par, bahut mote (obese) log pair cross nahi kar sakte. Jo auraten short skirts pehenti hain woh protection ke liye pair tightly cross karke baithti hain — lekin is se woh kam milansaar (less approachable) lagti hain.

## Why Kids Are Easier to Read
*(Bacche Padhne Mein Kyun Zyaada Aasaan Hote Hain)*

Bade umra ke log chote umar ke logon ke mukable padhne mein zyaada mushkil hote hain — kyunki unke chehere ki muscles ki kasaawat (tone) kam ho jaati hai.

**Paanch saal ka baccha:**
Jhooth bolta hai toh immediately ek ya dono haath apne muh par rakhta hai.

**Teenager:**
Haath muh ke paas aata hai — lekin saaf haath-se-muh-thaapne (obvious hand-slapping) ki bajaye, ungliyaan uske aas-paas halke se ghisti (rubbing) hain.

**Adult:**
Jab ek adult jhooth bolta hai — brain uske haath ko muh cover karne ka instruction deta hai — bilkul jaise 5-saal-ke bacche aur teenager ke waqt tha. Lekin last moment par, haath chehere se door ho jaata hai aur ek **Naak Chhoone Wala Gesture (Nose-Touch gesture)** aata hai. Yeh sirf adult version hai muh dhakne wale gesture ka jo bacchon mein hota hai.

Yeh dikhata hai ki jaise log bade hote hain, unke gestures zyaada baareek (subtle) aur kam spaasht (less obvious) ho jaate hain — isliye 50 saal ke bande ke gestures 5 saal ke bacche ke gestures se zyaada mushkil hote hain padhne mein.

## Can You Fake It?
*(Kya Body Language Ka Natak Kiya Ja Sakta Hai?)*

Hamse aksar poocha jaata hai: *"Kya body language fake ki ja sakti hai?"* Iska general jawab hai — **nahi** — kyunki main gestures, body ke bahut baareek signals (microsignals), aur bolne wale words ke beech mel (congruence) ki kami ho jaati hai.

Misal ke taur par, khuli hatheli (open palms) imaandaari (honesty) se associated hain — lekin jab ek faker apni palms bahar rakh ke tumse muskuraate hue jhooth bolta hai — uske bahut baareek gestures (micro-gestures) use pakad lete hain:
- Aankhon ki putali (pupils) chhoti (contract) ho sakti hain
- Ek bhaun upar ja sakti hai
- Ya muh ka kona pharak (twitch) kar sakta hai

Yeh signals khuli hatheli gesture aur sachchi muskurahat ko contradict karte hain — aur result yeh hota hai ki sunne wale — khaaskar auraten — jo suna usse believe nahi karti.

> **Body language mardoon ke saath fake karna auraton se zyaada aasaan hai — kyunki overall mard body language padhne mein achhe nahi hote.**

### Sach Ki Kahani: Jhooth Bolne Wala Job Applicant

Hum ek mard ka interview le rahe the jo explain kar raha tha ki usne apni pichli job kyun chodi. Usne kaha ki uske paas future opportunity nahi thi aur jaana mushkil tha kyunki woh sab staff ke saath achha tha.

Ek mahila saakshaatkarta (female interviewer) ne kaha ki use ek **andar se feel (intuitive feeling)** tha ki applicant jhooth bol raha hai aur uske pichle boss ke baare mein negative feelings hain — halanki applicant baar baar apne boss ki tarif kar raha tha.

Slow-motion video review mein humne notice kiya ki har baar jab applicant apne pichle boss ka zikr karta — ek pal ka naak-bhaun (split-second sneer) uske chehere ke left side par aata tha. Aksar yeh virodhabhaasi (contradictory) signals ek insaan ke chehere par pal ke ek ansh (fraction of a second) mein aate hain — aur ek ansikhsha (untrained) observer miss kar deta hai.

Hum ne pichle boss ko phone kiya aur pata chala — applicant ko doosre staff members ko drugs bechne ki wajah se nikaala gaya tha.

Key yahi hai — asli gestures ko nakli harakton (fake ones) se alag karo. **Aankhon ki putali ka phailna (pupil dilation), paseena, aur lali (blushing)** jaankaar taur par (consciously) fake nahi ki ja sakti — lekin khule haath dikhana seekha ja sakta hai.

> **Faker sirf thodi der tak hi fake kar sakte hain.**

Kuch cases mein body language jaankaar taur par (deliberately) fake ki jaati hai kuch fayde haasil karne ke liye. Misal ke taur par, Miss World ya Miss Universe contest mein, har pratiyogi (contestant) dikhaawe ki garmaahit (warmth) aur sacchaayi (sincerity) dene ke liye mehnat se seekhe hue body movements use karti hai. Lekin expert pratiyogiyaan bhi sirf thodi der tak hi body language fake kar sakti hain — aur eventually body aisi virodhabhaasi (contradictory) signals dene lagta hai jo jaankaar harakton (conscious actions) se alag hain.

Kaafi politicians body language fake karne mein mahir hote hain taaki voters unki baaton par yakeen karein — aur jo politicians yeh successfully kar lete hain — jaise John F. Kennedy aur Adolf Hitler — unhe **"charisma (akarshan)"** wala kaha jaata hai.

Khulaasa yeh hai ki lambe waqt tak body language fake karna mushkil hai — lekin yeh zaroori hai ki seekho ki positive body language kaise use karein aur negative body language kaise hataaein jo galat message de rahi ho.

## How to Become a Great Reader
*(Ek Achha Reader Kaise Banen)*

Roz kam se kam **15 minute** doosron ki body language study karne ke liye alag rakho — saath hi apne khud ke gestures ke baare mein bhi jaankaar chaukannata (conscious awareness) lao.

Padhne ke liye best jagah woh hain jahan log milte aur interact karte hain:
- **Airport** — khaaskar achha hai kyunki log openly utsaah (eagerness), gussa, dukh, khushi, bezaari (impatience), aur kaafi doosri emotions ko body language se express karte hain
- Social functions, business meetings, aur parties bhi excellent hain

Jab tum is art mein maahir (proficient) ho jaao — tum ek party mein jaake, corner mein poori shaam baith ke, sirf doosron ke body-language rituals dekhte hue ek exciting time guzaar sakte ho.

> **Modern humans apne poorvaj (ancestors) se body signals padhne mein zyaada kamzor ho gaye hain — kyunki ab hum words se bhatke hue (distracted) rehte hain.**

**TV dekhna bhi ek achha tarika hai seekhne ka:**
Awaaz band karo aur sirf picture dekhke samajhne ki koshish karo. Har kuch minute mein awaaz chalu karo aur check karo ki tera nonverbal reading kitna sahi tha. Jald hi poora program bina awaaz ke dekh paoge aur samajh jaoge — bilkul usi tarah jaise bahere (deaf) log karte hain.

Body-language signals padhna seekhna na sirf tumhe yeh zyaada samajhne deta hai ki doosre kaise dabaane (dominate) aur haathkaana (manipulate) karne ki koshish karte hain — balki yeh ehsaas bhi deta hai ki doosre bhi yahi hamare saath karte hain. Aur sabse important — yeh hume doosron ki feelings aur emotions ke baare mein zyaada samvedansheel (sensitive) banata hai.

Aaj hum ek naye qism ke social scientist ko dekhte hain — **Body Language Watcher (body language dekhne wala).** Bilkul jaise ek bird-watcher pakshiyon ko dekh ke enjoy karta hai — Body Language Watcher insaanon ke nonverbal cues aur signals dekhne mein anand leta hai — social functions mein, beaches par, TV par, office mein, ya kahi bhi jahan log interact karte hain.

Woh ek behaviour ka shagird (student) hai jo apne insaani saathiyon ke baare mein seekhna chahta hai — taaki woh aakhirkaar (ultimately) khud ke baare mein zyaada seekh sake aur apne rishton (relationships) ko behtar kar sake.
