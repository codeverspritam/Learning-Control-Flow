# Pudd’nhead Wilson — A Whisper to the Reader

### *(Paathak Ke Liye Ek Gupt Sandesh)*

---

> **TO THE READER.** *(Paathak Ke Liye)*
> *There is no character, howsoever good and fine, but it can be destroyed by ridicule, howsoever poor and witless. Observe the ass, for instance: his character is about perfect, he is the choicest spirit among all the humbler animals, yet see what ridicule has brought him to. Instead of feeling complimented when we are called an ass, we are left in doubt.—Pudd’nhead Wilson’s Calendar.*
> *(Aisa koi charitra [character] nahi hai, chahe woh kitna bhi achha aur umda kyun na ho, jise mazaak aur uphaas [ridicule] ke zariye tabaah na kiya ja sake, chahe woh mazaak kitna bhi ghatiya aur moorkhta-purna [witless] kyun na ho. Udaharan ke liye, gadhe [ass] ko dekhein: uska charitra lagbhag perfect hai, woh saare vinamra jaanwaaron mein sabse behad shreshth aatma hai, phir bhi dekho mazaak ne uski kya haalat kar di hai. Jab hamein koi gadha kehta hai, toh tareef mehsoos karne ke bajaye, hum shak mein pad jaate hain.)*

---

Ek aisa insaan jo kanooni mamlon (legal matters) se anjaan hai, usse hamesha galtiyan hone ki sambhavna rehti hai jab woh apne pen se kisi court ke scene ko portrait karne (photograph) ki koshish karta hai; aur isliye main is kitab mein kanoon ke chapters ko pehle ek trained barrister—agar unhein yahi kaha jata hai—dwara kade aur thaka dene wale vishleshan (rigid and exhausting revision) aur correction ke bina press mein bhejne ke liye taiyar nahi tha. Yeh chapters ab har ek detail mein sahi hain, kyunki unhein William Hicks ki seedhi nazar ke tehat dobara likha gaya hai, jinhone thirty-five saal pehle southwest Missouri mein kuch samay ke liye kanoon adhyayan kiya tha aur phir apni health ke liye yahan Florence aa gaye the aur abhi bhi Piazza del Duomo se kona ghoomte hi piche ki gali mein majood Macaroni Vermicelli ke ghode-ke-chare wale shed (horse-feed shed) mein exercise aur khane-peene ke badle madad kar rahe hain. Yeh theek us ghar ke thoda pare hai jahan woh patthar jisme Dante cheh sau saal pehle baitha karte the wall mein lagaya gya hai, jab woh aisa dikhava karte (let on) the ki woh unhein Giotto’s campanile (ghanta-ghar) banate hue dekh rahe hain aur phir bhi jaise hi Beatrice apna bachaav karne ke liye chestnut cake ka ek tukda lene ke raaste se guzarti thin (is darr se ki kahin school pahunche se pehle Ghibelline ka vidroh [outbreak] na ho jaye) toh woh dekhkar turant thak jaate the, usi purane stand par jahan woh aaj bhi wahi purani cake bechte hain aur yeh aaj bhi utni hi halki aur achhi hai jitni tab thi, aur yeh koi tareef (flattery) nahi hai, isse bahut door hai. Kanoon par unka haath thoda tang tha (He was a little rusty), lekin unhone is kitab ke liye abhyas kiya (rubbed up), aur woh do ya teen kanooni chapters ab poori tarah sahi aur seedhe hain. Unhone mujhe khud aisa bataya tha.

2 January, 1893 ko mere haathon dwara, Florence se teen miles piche, pahadiyon par sthit Settignano village ke Villa Viviani mein diya gaya—yeh jagah nishchit roop se is planet par majood sabse aakarshak nazaara (most charming view) pradan karti hai, aur iske sath hi kisi bhi planet ya yahan tak ki kisi bhi solar system mein dhoondhe jaane wale sabse khayali aur jaadui sunset (dreamlike and enchanting sunsets) pradan karti hai—aur ghar ke sabse shandaar room (swell room) mein diya gaya, jahan Cerretani senators aur is vansh ke doosre bade amiron (grandees) ke busts (aadhe sharir ki moortiyan) mujh par swikriti se neeche dekh rahi hain, jaise woh Dante par dekha karti thin, aur shaanti se mujhse pooch rahi hain ki main unhein apni family mein apna loon, jise main khushi ke sath karta hoon, kyunki mere sabse door ke poorvaj (remotest ancestors) in robed aur stately antiques ke samne abhi-abhi nikle nau-sikhia bache (spring chickens) jaise hain, aur yeh mere liye ek badi aur santoshjanak badhottari (satisfying lift) hogi, woh cheh sau saal ki badhottari hogi.

— Mark Twain
