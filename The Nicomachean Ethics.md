# INTRODUCTION

Aristotle pehle Western thinker the jinhone philosophy ko alag-alag branches mein divide kiya jo aaj bhi pehchane jaate hain: logic, metaphysics, natural philosophy, philosophy of mind, ethics aur politics, rhetoric; unhone in sabhi fields mein major contributions diye. Unka janam Stagira mein hua tha, jo northern Greece ka ek city hai, 384 BC mein. Unke father Nicomachus, Macedon ke Amyntas ke court mein ek doctor the, jo Philip se pehle the — Philip jinhone Greece ke bahut saare hisse conquer kiye the. Baad mein Aristotle Philip ke remarkable bete, Alexander the Great, ke tutor bane.

Jab Aristotle jawan the tab wo 367 mein Athens gaye Plato ke saath padhne ke liye, unki Academy mein, aur wahin rahe jab tak 347 mein Plato ki death nahi ho gayi. Ye wo period tha jab Plato ne Timaeus, Sophist, Statesman, aur Philebus jaisi works likhi, saath hi apni Laws bhi likhi, aur ye sochna interesting (intriguing) hai ki inme se kitna kuch unke brilliant student Aristotle ke challenges ki wajah se aaya hoga. Asia Minor mein kuch waqt bitane ke baad (jahan shayad unhone apne biological works ke liye research ki thi) aur phir Macedon wapas tutor ke roop mein, Aristotle Athens laut aaye aur apna khud ka philosophical school, Lyceum, banaya: iske kuch remains recently modern Athens mein discover hue hain. Unki death 322 BC mein hui, us saal ke baad jab unhe Athens chhodna pada apne former student, emperor Alexander, ki death ke baad.

Kuch popular works ke fragments ko chhod kar, jo writings humein mili hain wo academic treatises hain, kuch zyada polished, kuch kam. Ye modern sense mein publish nahi hui thi, aur ho sakta hai Aristotle inhe revise karte rahe hon. Lyceum mein padhaai ki breadth aur character ko in surviving treatises se andaza lagaya ja sakta hai, jo ek immense range cover karti hain, aur hamesha ek questioning, argumentative (bahas karne wali), aur non-dogmatic (bina zid ke) style mein likhi gayi hain. Ho sakta hai ye works jaisi survive hui hain wo lecture-notes hi hon, aur isi se inki kuch rough features samajh aati hain. Sach ye hai ki humein inke original form aur purpose ke baare mein bahut kam pata hai, ki inhe kis order mein likha gaya tha ya kaise edit kiya gaya.

Ethics par teen works Aristotle ke naam se aayi hain: Nicomachean Ethics (NE) das 'books' mein, Eudemian Ethics (EE) aath 'books' mein, aur so-called Magna Moralia ya 'great ethics'. Ye aakhri wali shayad Aristotle ki khud ki nahi hai balki kisi student ka lecture course record ho sakta hai. Baaki do ke titles unke editors se aaye maane jaate hain jo Aristotle ki death ke baad aaye: Nicomachus Aristotle ke bete the, jabki Eudemus ek student the. Dono works kaafi same topics cover karti hain, aur inke beech ka relation controversial hai, saath hi ek added puzzle bhi hai ki teen books dono works mein common hain: NE ki Books V-VII, EE ki Books IV-VI se correspond karti hain. Zyadatar scholarly opinion ye hai ki Nicomachean Ethics baad ki work hai, aur ye kam se kam first ya second century AD se Aristotle ki ethics par major aur definitive work maani jaati rahi hai. Kuch hisse doosron se zyada polished hain, aur ek common book (Book V, justice par) mein kuch material out of place lagta hai. Aristotle ki writing mein ek certain terse (sanchit/kam shabdon wali) elegance hai, aur ye arguments present karne ke liye ideal hai, jinse unki philosophy bhari padi hai. Ye work happiness ki discussion se shuru hoti hai, phir moral virtues — character ke virtues — ki taraf jaati hai, justice sameit; aur phir intellect ke virtues ki taraf; ye pleasure aur friendship, aur best life mein inke role ki discussion karti hai, phir happiness ki further discussion par wapas aati hai, aur final transition political theory ki taraf hoti hai. Aristotle ethics ko politics ki ek branch maante hain, aur unki work Politics isi ka sequel ban kar design hui thi.

Plato, aur unke teacher Socrates, ne Aristotle ki philosophizing ke liye scene set kiya. 399 BC mein death ki saza paayi, Socrates ne koi written philosophy nahi chhodi aur humein unke views Plato ke dialogues se, saath hi Xenophon ki works se, samajhne padte hain. Aristotle, jinhone Socrates ki death ke chaalis saal baad Plato ke saath padhna shuru kiya, Socrates ko ethical questions mein exclusive interest ka credit dete hain, aur unhe kuch key theses attribute karte hain: ki saari virtues ek tarah ka knowledge hain (VI. 13) aur ki koi bhi wo cheez ke against act nahi karta jo wo best jaanta (ya judge karta) hai (VII. 2). Socrates dwara, aur unke baad Plato dwara, discuss kiye gaye ethical questions ye hain: insaan ko kaise jeena chahiye; virtues kya hain, kya inhe sikhaya ja sakta hai, aur sabse zyada, ye kyun choose karne layak hain. 'The unexamined life is not worth living' (bina jaanche-parkhe zindagi jeene layak nahi), Socrates apni Apology mein declare karte hain — wo speech jo Plato ne likhi thi, unke trial mein unki defence hone ka daava karte hue. Isme Socrates apni lifestyle describe karte hain jisme wo so-called experts se sawaal-jawaab karte hain ye dekhne ke liye ki kya wo apne beliefs ko defend kar sakte hain.

Aristotle ki zyada tar philosophy unke predecessors (pehle wale thinkers) ki writings par ek reflection aur response hai, aur wo metaphysics, natural philosophy, aur philosophy of mind par unke views se khud ko door rakhna chahte hain. Lekin major ethical questions par, Socrates, Plato, aur Aristotle agreement mein hain — halaanki Aristotle kabhi bhi khule shabdon mein ye admit nahi karte. Teeno agree karte hain ki insaano ke liye highest good happiness hai, aur ki life ka ek rational choice apni khud ki happiness ki taraf directed hoga. Sirf wahi life happy hogi jisme insaan traditional virtues (justice, temperance, courage, aur practical wisdom) cultivate karta hai. Plato ke dialogues mein Socrates ko traditional virtues ke against kuch immoralist challenges face karte dikhaya gaya hai, lekin morality par sceptical attacks ki treatment Aristotle ke work mein zyadatar absent hai. Unke major contributions mein happiness kya hai iska ek in-depth study, virtues happiness se kaise related hain, aur different types ke virtue — moral aur intellectual — kya hain, shaamil hai. Wo Socrates dwara knowledge ko diye gaye prominence par sawaal uthate hain moral virtue ke account mein, lekin unka apna considered view phronēsis, practical wisdom, ko moral virtue mein ek key role deta hai, aur wo Socrates se is baat mein agree karte hain ki moral virtues essentially united hain (VI. 13). Aristotle apne teacher Plato ko ek famous chapter mein challenge karte hain jisme Plato ke Form of the Good ki discussion hoti hai (1.6), saath hi ye bhi admit karte hain ki 'ye inquiry ek uphill (mushkil/chadhaai wali) hai, kyunki Forms ko humare apne dosto ne hi admit kiya hai.' Ye insist karte hue ki inquiry ke different subjects ke different starting points hote hain aur alag approaches chahiye, Aristotle ek so-called universal good, ya good ka 'Form' ya 'Idea', jo Plato ki Republic mein sab kuch ki goodness aur being ke foundation ke roop mein introduce kiya gaya tha, ko dismiss karte hain. Wo ise ek impossible concept mante hain aur ethics ke liye anyway irrelevant bhi, jo insaan ke good ka study hai.

Plato ne, saath hi doosre predecessors ne bhi, pleasure aur goodness ke relation ko lambe mein explore kiya tha. Ek dialogue, Protagoras, mein Plato dikhate hain ki Socrates hedonism defend kar rahe hain, jo ye thesis hai ki pleasure hi good hai, Protagoras ki zyada conventional morality ke against. Aristotle ka contribution is debate mein ye tha ki wo pleasure kya hai aur humein isse kaise sochna chahiye, is sawaal mein bahut zyada gehraai mein gaye, saath hi ye bhi pucha ki ye good se kaise related hai. Best life mein pleasure ke sthaan (place) ke sawaal par unka jawaab Plato ki Republic aur Philebus jaisa hi hai: pleasure good nahi hai, lekin best life zaroor sabse pleasant bhi hogi, jisme wo pleasures shaamil honge jo humari rational beings ki nature ke suited hain. Isi tarah Aristotle ethics ke aur bhi central concepts, jaise voluntariness, choice, deliberation, aur practical reasoning, mein bahut gehraai se probe (jaanch) karte hain; inme se kuch neeche detail mein discuss kiye gaye hain.

## Aristotle's Ethical Theory: Its Key Elements

### The Human Good: Happiness

Aristotle ke liye, ethics insaan ke good ka inquiry hai. Action se attain hone wale sabhi goods mein sabse highest kya hai? Har koi — educated aur lowly, healthy aur sick — (wo likhte hain) is baat par verbal agreement rakhte hain: ye happiness hai, eudaimonia (1.4). Aur sab isse doing well ya faring well ke saath equate karte hain. Lekin happiness kya hai, ye ek purani (long-standing) dispute ka matter hai, humein pata chalta hai, jisme teen 'lives' contention mein hain: sensual enjoyment ki, political achievement ki, aur intellectual contemplation ki (1.5). Aristotle best life ke liye ek chautha (fourth) contender bhi add karte hain, lekin usse jaldi dismiss kar dete hain: wealth ka pursuit. Ise dismiss karne ke liye unhe sirf ye point out karna hota hai ki wealth kisi aur cheez ke liye chaahi jaati hai. Highest good ko khud ke liye chaha jaana chahiye; ye ek activity mein consist karna chahiye (kisi state mein nahi jisme insaan hota hai) aur self-sufficient hona chahiye, kisi cheez ki kami na ho. Ye sab confirm karta hai ki happiness, jo in conditions ko satisfy karti hai, sach mein highest good hai. Lekin ek zyada informative jawaab paane ke liye, wo ye idea invoke karte hain ki insaan ka ek function hai — rational activity — aur conclude karte hain ki happiness excellent rational activity hai: unke shabdon mein, virtue ke according rational activity (1.7). 'Function' ergon ka translation hai, literally 'task' ya 'work'. Hum neeche 'function argument' par, aur excellence aur virtue par wapas aayenge.

Ab tak humein modern reader ke liye kaafi kuch surprising mila hai. Ethics happiness ka study kyun ho, aur — shayad — is baat ka nahi ki main dusron ka kya lena hoon, ya right action ka criterion kya hai? Hum is sawaal par neeche wapas aayenge. Happiness ko outstanding rational activity define karna un logo ko confusing (puzzling) lag sakta hai jo maante hain ki happiness ek mental state hai, subjective well-being ki ek state. Is problem ko aasaan karne ke liye, kuch logo ne suggest kiya hai ki eudaimonia ko 'flourishing' (phalna-phoolna) ya 'fulfilment' translate karna chahiye. Clearly 'happiness' se Aristotle kisi bhi tarah ki mental state ki baat nahi kar rahe, aur bilkul bhi aisi state ki nahi jahan subjects ke self-reports invite kiye jaayein aur definitive maane jaayein.

Happiness ki apni investigation mein, yani insaano ke liye best life ki, Aristotle kai assumptions banate hain. Jawaab kisi individual ki preferences ke hisaab se vary nahi karega; aur logo ka apni khud ki happiness ka assessment galat ho sakta hai. Happiness ko pleasure se equate nahi kiya jaana chahiye, lekin phir bhi, wo (Book X mein) pleasure aur highest good ke beech ke is puraane sawaal ko solve karenge ye find karke ki truly happy life sach mein sabse pleasant bhi hogi, halaanki uska highest good hona uski pleasantness ki wajah se nahi hai. Happiness sirf unhe available hai jinki age, gender, aur civic status unhe excellent activities ki wo life pursue karne dete hain jo isse banaati hain. Bacchon ko happy sirf is sense mein bulaya ja sakta hai ki unki lives happiness ka promise karti hain; slaves ki life happiness ko preclude (rok deti) karti hai, aur — hum shayad infer kar sakte hain — waisi hi women ki bhi, halaanki ye unsaid chhoda gaya hai. Sabse zyada striking shayad unka famous 'function argument' ka use hai.

### Happiness and Human Function: Rational Activity

'Function argument' ka use insaan ke good ko uske human function ke through find karne ke liye kiya jaata hai (1.7). Ye ek bure start se shuru hota hai, function rakhne walon ke examples ke saath — flute-players, aankhein, haath, ya paanv — jo irrelevant lagte hain. Flute-players, apne hi title se, wo log hain jinka role flute bajaana hai, aur isse insaano ke liye, as such, kisi role ke baare mein kuch follow nahi hota. Aur halaanki hum aasaani se agree kar sakte hain ki aankh ka function dekhna hai, ye is wajah se hai kyunki poore organism mein yahi iska role hai. Ye parallels humein convince nahi karenge ki insaano ka bhi ek function hai. Balki, Aristotle apni nature ki philosophy se ek key assumption draw kar rahe hain. Insaano ke jeene ka ek tarika hona chahiye, aur unhe kaise jeena chahiye. Ye is wajah se nahi hai ki god ne unhe kisi purpose ke liye banaya — jo Aristotle nahi maante the — balki simply is wajah se ki wo ek certain kism ke living being hain, aur har living species ka apna work ya function hota hai. Insaano ke paas kai capacities hain — Aristotle inhe soul ki capacities kehte hain, lekin soul se unka matlab sirf itna hai jiski wajah se koi cheez alive hoti hai. Kuch capacities lower animals ke saath share hoti hain; reason wo capacity hai jo insaan ko alag banaati hai. To, kyunki kisi being ka function wo hai jo special hai, na ki jo shared hai, reason hi best human life ki key hai.

Rational capacities ko aur investigate karne se pehle, chaliye kuch objections par ruk jaate hain. Pehla, hum ye kyun infer karein ki insaano ko kya karna best hai, sirf isliye ki wo kya kar sakte hain? Bahut saari cheezein hain jo sirf insaan, apni rational capacities ki wajah se, kar sakte hain. Jaise cards mein cheat karna, mass destruction ke weapons banana, ya bacchon ko sex-abuse ke liye groom karna: hum inhe best human life ka part nahi banana chahenge. Ya phir, hum un activities ko best life se kyun exclude karein jo hum doosre animals ke saath share karte hain, jaise offspring ki parwarish karna? (Iska jawaab aasan hai, ye recognize karke ki reason use karke aisa karna, animal ki tarah karne se different maana ja sakta hai.) Sabse zyada serious sawaal ye hai ki insaano ka ek general function insist kyun kiya jaaye? Insaano ke baare mein jo special hai wo shayad ye hai ki individuals apni abilities, preferences, aur goals mein kitne markedly (spasht roop se) alag hain.

Kuch log ek alag objection uthate hain: kisi insaan ke liye good, ek good person jo typically karta hai, usme kyun consist ho? Wo Aristotle par ye ilzaam lagate hain ki wo good ke matlab ko confuse (conflate) kar rahe hain — beneficial wale sense mein (yani insaano ke liye kya good hai), ek different, shayad moral good ke saath (yani ek good person kya karta hai) — jab wo declare karte hain ki kisi F (ek flute-player, ek insaan) ke liye good wahi hoga jo good F karta hai. Lekin ye koi real difficulty nahi hai. Human good dhoondte hue, Aristotle wo good dhoondh rahe hain jo insaano ke concern mein hai, na ki kisi aisi cheez ki jo insaano ke liye good ho, jaise khaana aur paani unke liye good hote hain. Aur jab wo ek good human being ki baat karte hain, unhone illicitly (galat tareeke se) ek morally good person ka notion smuggle nahi kiya hai. Ek good person, itna to bas, ek good specimen hai insaan ka, jaise ek good oak tree ya ek good elephant. Lekin phir moral goodness aur virtues ka kya, ye picture mein kaise aate hain?

### Excellent Rational Activity and the Virtues

Ab tak ek immoralist jaisa Thrasymachus, Plato ki Republic mein, Aristotle ki happiness ki definition se agree kar sakta hai — ek life jo excellence ya virtue (aretē) ke according rational activity hai. Thrasymachus, injustice ki life ki tareef karte hue, yani doosron ko exploit karne aur unse aage nikalne ki, ise ek virtue kehta hai aur ise rational aur sensible bata kar praise karta hai. To key questions ye hain: reason aur rationality kya hain, aur inhe exercise karne ka kaunsa tareeka excellence ya virtue manifest karta hai? Yahan humein translation ki ek problem milti hai. Aretē, jise usually virtue translate kiya jaata hai, iska matlab kisi bhi tarah ki excellence hai, aur ye pruning-hooks (kaatne ke औzaar) par bhi apply ho sakta hai, insaano par bhi. Jaisa abhi note kiya gaya, Plato ka character Thrasymachus deny kar sakta hai ki justice ek virtue hai, simply is wajah se ki wo ise ek excellent quality nahi maanta. To Aristotle apne virtues ke selection ko — excellent rational activities ke selection ko — kaise justify karte hain?

Reason, jaisa hum 1.13 mein seekhte hain, human soul mein different tareekon se feature karta hai. (Jab wo soul ki baat karte hain, jaisa humne dekha, Aristotle ka matlab simply kisi living cheez ki capacities se hai, jab tak wo alive hai.) Ek soul-part, intellect, mein reason full sense mein hota hai, lekin jo part appetites (chahaton) rakhta hai, wo ek secondary tareeke se rational hai. Yani, appetites reason ke responsive hote hain, halaanki khud rational nahi hote. Hum inhe semi-rational label kar sakte hain, halaanki Aristotle ise is tarah nahi rakhte. Kyunki do soul-parts hain, apne alag tareeko se rational, isliye do kism ki virtue hoti hain, character ki virtues — jinka locus appetites hain, aur intellect ki virtues. Moral virtues, ya character ki virtues, ki discussion mein sabse zyada hissa hoga (II–V), jabki VI intellect ki virtues discuss karti hai, jinme se ek vital link moral virtues ke saath, practical wisdom, hai. Character ki virtues ke account mein, humein milta hai ki traditional virtues — courage, temperance, aur justice — jaldi hi discussion mein aa jaati hain, halaanki — jaisa humne dekha — ek morally good person ka notion happiness ki definition mein pehle se implicit nahi tha. Jo hum vain (bekaar) mein dhoondte hain wo ek argument hai ki apni rationality ko sabse best possible tareeke se, 'excellence ke according', exercise karna, traditional moral virtues ko rakhna aur exercise karna hai. Shayad Aristotle ne socha ki aisa proof possible nahi tha; aur kyunki unke audience well-brought-up (achhe se pale-badhe) jawaan log the (lekin bahut zyada jawaan nahi!), ye zaroori bhi nahi tha.

Aristotle ke account mein, virtues ke baare mein kya valuable hai, chahe wo intellect ki virtues ho ya moral virtues, chahe self-regarding (khud se related) ho jaise temperance, ya other-regarding (dusron se related) jaise justice? Unke system mein, unhe virtues banane wali cheez simply ye hai ki, unhe rakh kar aur exercise kar ke, insaan ek life jee raha hota hai jo ek insaan ke liye best life hai. Wo ek person ki eudaimonia mein intrinsically contribute karti hain. (Wo ye allow karte hain ki kuch external goods bhi eudaimonia ke liye necessary conditions hain, ek view par attack karte hue — shayad unhone ise Plato ka view maana — ki virtue hi sufficient hai.) Ise ek consequentialist view se contrast karein jisme human virtues society ya humare neighbours ya khud humare liye jo results laate hain unki wajah se valuable maani jaati hain. Main is contrast par neeche wapas aaunga.

### How Moral Virtues are Acquired and How they 'Lie in a Mean'

Aristotle insist karte hain ki habituation (aadat dalna), na ki teaching, moral virtue ka raasta hai (II. 1). Humein good actions karne ki practice karni hoti hai, sirf virtue ke baare mein padhna kaafi nahi. Halaanki important tarike se sahi, ye oversimplify (zyada simple bana dena) karta hai, aur jaldi hi clear ho jaata hai ki reason ka bhi ek role hai. Jabki moral virtues semi-rational soul part ki excellences hain jisme appetites (emotions samet) hote hain, virtue proper banne ke liye, reason ke responsiveness ki zaroorat hoti hai. Reason ke responsiveness ke saath-saath, ek virtuous person good actions karne mein enjoy karne lagta hai (II.3), aur sahi feelings develop karta hai (fear, anger, waghera ke).

Ab hum Aristotle ke us famous doctrine par aate hain ki moral virtue ek tarah ka mean (madhya/beech ka point) hai. Ek moral virtue rakhna matlab 'ek intermediate tareeke se' feel karne aur act karne ke liye disposed hona; virtues 'mean' ya 'intermediate' states hain. Humein ise 'har cheez mein moderation' ka doctrine nahi samajhna chahiye. Balki, isme feelings rakhna (jaise anger ka) aur responses jo 'intermediate' hain — appropriate ya proportional ke sense mein — chahiye hote hain. Halaanki Aristotle ise excess aur defect, zyada aur kam, se bachne ke roop mein characterize karte hain, sach mein ye idea kuch misleading hai, kyunki har galat hone ka tareeka zyada ya kam involve nahi karta. Zyada helpful ye characterization hai ki intermediate wo hai jo best hai, aur 'sahi time par, sahi objects ke reference mein, sahi logo ke prati, sahi motive ke saath, aur sahi tareeke se' karna aur feel karna hai (II.6). Unki eventual definition ek moral virtue ki ye hai ki ye 'ek state of character hai jo choice se concerned hai, ek mean mein lying, yani humare relative mean, jo reason se determine kiya jaata hai, aur us reason se jise practical wisdom ka aadmi determine karega (II.b)'. Ab tak unhone feelings aur actions ke roles outline kiye hain, aur moral virtue ko ek acquired state of character ke roop mein identify kiya hai jo humein appropriately feel karne aur act karne ke liye choose karne ko dispose karta hai. Jaisa definition ka last clause reveal karta hai, isse account mein ek gap reh jaata hai, jo tab fill hoga jab wo intellect ki virtues discuss karenge, jinme se practical wisdom (phronēsis) sabse prominent hai.

### Virtue Proper and Continence

Do soldiers imagine karein. Ek reasonably fearless hai bina ek dare-devil (khatarnaak roop se nirbhay) hue, usne ye samajh develop ki hai ki kaunse dangers face kiye jaane chahiye, aur wo unhe just appropriate degree ka fear feel karte hue face kar sakta hai. Uska comrade-in-arms (saathi sipahi) different hai; terrors (bhayankar dar) se pareshan hote hue bhi wo apni post hold karta hai aur battle mein wo role play karta hai jiski usse expectation hai. Pehla, na ki doosra, soldier courage ki moral virtue ka credit paata hai, Aristotle ke according. Ya phir, teen citizens imagine karein, jinke paas city ki pleasures tak access hai: achha khaana aur wine, sex ke opportunities. Citizen A inhe appropriate matra mein desire aur enjoy karta hai, due reflection ke saath lekin kisi conflict ki feeling ke bina. B utna hi indulge karta hai jitna A, lekin usse aksar apni over-strong physical pleasures ki appetites ko rein (kaabu) mein rakhna padta hai, jabki C ko pata hai ki usse aisa karna chahiye, lekin kabhi-kabhi wo succumb (haar maan) ho jaata hai aur zyada indulge karta hai jitna usse pata hai ki usse nahi karna chahiye. In sabme se, A ke paas temperance ki virtue hai, jabki B sirf 'continent' ya self-controlled (enkratēs) hai, aur C 'incontinent' hai, doosre shabdon mein self-control ki kami hai halaanki abhi vicious nahi hai, kyunki usse realize hota hai ki usse aise indulge nahi karna chahiye jaise wo karta hai. Aristotle ka verdict, ki A hi morally virtuous hai na ki B, ek aise ethical outlook ko perverse, aur sach mein shocking, laga hai jo Christianity se derive hota hai jo temptation ko overcome karne ko value karta hai. Lekin humein yaad rakhna hoga ki moral virtue, yani character ki excellence, wo best state of character hai jo koi insaan rakh sakta hai. Agar, chah kar, aap ye bana sakte ki aapka godson in mein se ek ban jaaye, aap zaroor chahenge ki wo pehla ho, doosra nahi, aur phir, A ki tarah, unruly ya over-powerful appetites se free ho, na ki wo jise unhe curb karna pade. Humein continence ki koi extended discussion nahi milti, lekin iska opposite — incontinence — ki lambi discussion milti hai, kyunki ye Aristotle ki moral psychology mein ek apparent problem paida karti hai (neeche discuss kiya gaya hai).

### The Virtues of Intellect and Practical Wisdom

Happiness ke account ke liye intellect ki virtues (Book VI) ki discussion zaroori hai, do reasons se. Pehla, virtues ke roop mein, ye best life ke liye zaroori hain: happy hone ke liye insaan ko sochne aur reason karne mein in virtues ko employ karna hota hai. Doosra, jaisa humne dekha, moral virtue ki definition mein reason ka ek essential reference tha, yani, wo reason jo phronimos (practical wisdom wala insaan) kisi bhi diye gaye instance mein virtuous act kya hai ye determine karne ke liye use karta hai. (Brevity ke liye, main aage Greek term, phronimos, use karunga.) To humein khaas kar phronēsis, practical wisdom, ki discussion chahiye.

Theoretical aur practical thinking ke beech ka distinction highlight karte hue, Aristotle ise (VI. 1) necessary truths, jaise mathematics ki, aur contingent truths, jaise ye ki kal sea-battle hogi ya nahi, ke beech ke distinction ke saath align karte hain. Theoretical side par unhe do virtues milti hain, scientific knowledge aur intuitive reason, jo saath mein wisdom (sophia) banaati hain (VI.7). Kyunki scientific knowledge ko proof chahiye hota hai, aur kisi bhi proof ko unproven assumptions se shuru hona padta hai, intuitive reason (nous) chahiye hota hai un starting points ko grasp karne ke liye jo deductive reasoning ke liye zaroori hain jo scientific knowledge require karti hai. Practical side par (aise matters se deal karte hue jo otherwise ho sakte hain, isliye deliberation ke liye suitable hain) wo 'making' — art ki province (yani kisi outcome ko produce karne ki expertise) — aur 'doing', jahan doing ke alawa koi outcome aim nahi kiya jaata, ke beech ek important distinction draw karte hain (VI.5). Practical wisdom (phronēsis) doing se concerned intellectual virtue hai.

Is complex discussion se unke account mein kuch tensions reveal hoti hain: kya phronimos ko general principles chahiye, ya particular cases mein sahi hona kaafi hai? Wo kitna deliberate aur reason karta hai, ya phronēsis zyada tar kisi bhi situation mein jo action call karti hai uske salient features ko 'dekhne' ka matter hai? Ye doosra wala certainly phronēsis ki ek key feature hai. VI ke last do chapters mein aur important points banaye gaye hain, jo phronēsis aur moral virtue ke close connection ko reinforce karte hain. 'Virtue goal ko correct banati hai aur practical wisdom us tak le jaane wale raaste ko correct banati hai': pehli nazar mein ye suggest karta hai ki labour ki ek problematic division hai, aur ki practical wisdom ka role sirf means-end reasoning se zyada kuch nahi hai. Lekin ye poori picture nahi ho sakti jo Aristotle paint karna chahte hain. Kyunki, jab tak reason kisi ke emotional development ko guide na kare, wo pehli jagah moral virtue nahi rakhega. Book VI ke end tak, humein pata chalta hai ki virtues ka initial division moral aur intellectual mein kuch misleading tha. Phronēsis, halaanki ek intellectual virtue hai, moral virtues se independently develop nahi ho sakti, jabki wo, apni taraf se, halaanki person ke non-rational (ya, jaisa humne kaha, semi-rational) part ki virtues hain, sirf reason ki guidance mein hi apni perfection tak pahunch sakti hain.

### The Final Account of Happiness: Contemplation

Book X mein, initial question par wapas aate hue, Aristotle likhte hain: 'Agar happiness virtue ke according activity hai, to reasonable hai ki ye highest virtue ke according ho; aur ye humare andar ki best cheez ki hogi.' Jaisa wo aage argue karte hain, humare andar ki best cheez reason ka ek aspect hai, na ki reason overall. Ye wo aspect hai jo unchanging objects aur necessary truths ka study karta hai; highest activity contemplation hai, aur iska virtue wisdom (sophia) hai, us special sense mein jo ise theoretical, yani philosophical, thinking ki excellence tak confine karta hai. Ek baar phir wo criteria invoke karte hue jo unhone I.7 mein best good ke liye laid down kiye the, wo ye dikhane ki koshish karte hain ki contemplative life sabse zyada end-like aur sabse self-sufficient hai, aur moral virtue ki life ko in dono respects mein (aur doosron mein bhi) surpass karti hai. Kya ye ek emphasis ka change signal karta hai, ya kya Aristotle ne Book I mein hi apni eventual declaration ke liye zameen taiyaar kar di thi ki contemplative life happiest hai, jabki moral virtue ki life sirf secondary tareeke se happiest hai?

Ye ek intense scholarly dispute ka matter hai, aur resolve karna mushkil hai. Interpretation ka ek school Aristotle ko 1.7 mein firmly ek inclusive account of happiness advocate karte hue paata hai, aise ki best life un goods ka best combination include karegi jinhe hum khud ke liye desire karte hain. Sirf isi tarah ye 'doosre goods ke addition se zyada desirable nahi banayi ja sakti'. Lekin, agar Aristotle ne Book I mein aisa inclusive account favour kiya, to ye clash karta lagta hai sirf ek kism ki activity, contemplation, ko best select karne se, aur moral virtue ki practice (uske saathi, practical wisdom, ke saath) ko second place mein daalne se. Interpretation ki doosri line note karti hai ki, happiness ek inclusive end hai wale indications ke saath-saath, Book I ne pehle se hint diya tha ki happiness best activities mein se best ke saath identify hogi (1099a30). Phir bhi, kaam ka zyada tar hissa moral virtues aur action ki philosophy ke related issues, jaise voluntariness, choice, aur deliberation, par focus raha hai. Aisa hone se, reader ko contemplation ki life ki final tareef (X.7 aur 8), aur morally virtuous action ki value ko downplay kiya jaana, surprising lagega.

Practical matters par Aristotle ka focus, phir bhi, final chapter mein wapas aata hai. Apne initial statement ke sach rehte hue ki ethics politics ki ek branch hai, wo poochte hain ki morally good behaviour, aur wo dispositions (moral virtues) jo ise prompt karti hain, kaise best develop ki ja sakti hain. Parents dwara ordinary upbringing ke alawa, good laws bahut saare reasons ke liye essential hain. Laws child-rearing ke kuch aspects ordain karti hain; wo good behaviour ke standards set karti hain, aur log tab behtar respond karte hain jab laws, na ki despots (tanashaah), standards impose karne ki koshish karti hain. To ethics ka ek full study mein law ki, aur best type ki constitution ki, discussions shaamil karni hongi jo wo apni Politics mein aage denge.

## Aristotle's Ethics and Alternative Approaches

Jo log Aristotle ko padhte hain aur ethics mein kuch aur important approaches se familiar hain, wo zaroor poochenge ki ye theories kaise compare hoti hain. Kuch log to yahan tak claim karte hain ki Aristotle jo hum aaj morality samajhte hain wo discuss hi nahi kar rahe. Main briefly do zyada recent, aur famous, ethical theories ko touch karta hoon, aur phir ek newer approach dekhta hoon, so-called neo-Aristotelian Virtue Theory.

### Kant

Kant ki moral theory unki Groundwork of the Metaphysic of Morals mein adumbrate (rooprekha di gayi) ki gayi hai. (Ek baad ki work, Metaphysic of Morals, Groundwork mein liye gaye positions ko develop aur kuch tareeko se mitigate (halka) karti hai.) Iske key tenets mein ye idea shaamil hai ki 'good will' hi ek unconditionally good cheez hai aur ki moral worth rakhne ke liye actions duty ke motive se kiye jaane chahiye. Emotions, feelings, aur inclinations, benevolent (bhale) bhi ho to, action ke moral worth mein kuch contribute nahi karte. Na hi actual na hi intended consequences kisi action ko koi moral worth de sakte hain, sirf duty ke liye kiya jaana hi de sakta hai. Famously wo likhte hain: 'Mujhe kabhi bhi aise act nahi karna chahiye siwaye is tarah se ki main ye bhi will kar sakoon ki mera maxim ek universal law ban jaaye.' Ek alag formulation mein, wo insist karte hain ki insaan ko humesha humanity ko sirf means ke roop mein nahi balki khud mein ek end ke roop mein bhi treat karna chahiye.

Is bahut brief sketch se bhi Aristotle ki theory se kuch seemingly sharp contrasts evident hain. Kant ke liye 'good will' unconditionally good cheez hai, Aristotle ke liye happiness. Jabki Aristotle assume karte hain ki apni khud ki happiness hi wo end hai jise aim karna rational hai, aur yahi wo cheez hai jisse phronimos concerned hai, Kant to yahan tak deny karte hain ki apni khud ki happiness jise wo 'pure practical reason' kehte hain uska koi proper concern honi chahiye. Ye bina shaq iska ek hissa is wajah se hai kyunki Kant happiness ke apne understanding mein Aristotle se different the. Aristotle ke liye virtuous aadmi wo hai jo apni good actions ko enjoy karta hai aur jiske paas appropriate feelings hain sahi act karne ke saath-saath. Iske contrast mein, Kant — effectively — us person ko moral worth dete hain jisse Aristotle sirf 'continent' kehte hain, kyunki (Kant ke account mein) jo matter karta hai wo ye hai ki kya agent duty se motivated hai, na ki unki feelings kya hain. Kant ye allow nahi kar sakte ki moral worth kisi bhi tarah non-rational appetites ya inclinations par depend kare. Aur halaanki dono thinkers ethical life mein reason ke role par important stress dete hain, ye har ek mein kaafi different form leta hai. Universality, Kant ke liye ek maxim ki morality ki hallmark hai. Aristotle, phir bhi, practical wisdom ke apne account mein, circumstances ki particularity par zyada emphasis dete hain, aur is baat par ki phronimos ko har case mein ethically salient features 'dekhna' zaroori hai.

### Consequentialism

Consequentialist theories, jinme utilitarianism sabse famous version hai, ek bahut different form leti hain. Jeremy Bentham aur J. S. Mill — jo Aristotle ke ek close reader the — utilitarianism ke sabse famous advocates hain. Jaisa humne dekha, Kant ki theory duty ke motive par emphasis deti hai aur consequences ko koi role deny karti hai — isi wajah se ise deontological theory maana jaata hai. Consequentialism, jaisa naam se pata chalta hai, actions ke consequences ko unki rightness ke liye ek hi relevant feature maanta hai. Utilitarianism ke liye, kisi action ko right banane wali cheez ye hai ki ye wo action hai (agent ke liye available sab actions mein se) jo general happiness ko maximize karta hai. Jahan tak ye maanta hai ki happiness hi ek intrinsically valuable cheez hai, ye Aristotle ki theory ke zyada close lagta hai. Lekin is baare mein kam se kam do major differences hain. Pehla, Mill happiness ko ek mental state, pleasure, se equate karte hain (halaanki pleasure ke evaluation mein quantity ke saath quality ko bhi admit karte hain). Aur zyada important, utilitarianism insist karti hai ki sab logo ki happiness — sentient non-humans (jaanwar) sameit — hi, na ki sirf agent ki happiness, right action ka criterion hai. Mill likhte hain, 'apni khud ki happiness aur dusron ki happiness ke beech utilitarianism usse ek disinterested aur benevolent spectator ki tarah strictly impartial hone ki demand karti hai' (Utilitarianism, ch. 2). Aur jabki ek consequentialist theory bahut saari virtues ko value karti hai aur unke cultivation ko encourage karti hai, ye unhe sirf instrumentally valuable maanti hai. Yani, ye us wajah se valuable hain jo consequences ye laane mein help karti hain, kyunki virtues right actions karne ki propensities (pravriti) hain, yani wo jo general happiness ko maximize karti hain.

Apne enormous differences ke bawajood, Kantian aur consequentialist ethical theories kuch features share karti hain. Dono primarily is baat se concerned hain ki actions ko right kya banata hai (ya, Kant ke terms mein, actions ko moral worth kya deta hai). Dono seemingly impartiality, ek certain disinterestedness, aur apne concerns se ek detachment require karti hain. Iska matlab ye nahi ki in theories ke liye morality sirf dusron ke saath humare relations ka matter hai: Kant maante hain ki khud ke prati bhi duties hoti hain, aur, consequentialism mein, agent ki apni happiness kisi aur ki jitni hi important hai, na kam na zyada. Lekin dono theories disinterestedness aur impartiality par ek aisa emphasis deti hain jo Aristotle ke us approach se sharply contrast karta hai jise hum agent-centred approach keh sakte hain. Halaanki unki theory kisi bhi tarah se narrowly egoistic nahi hai, ye certainly ego-centred zaroor hai.

### Neo-Aristotelian Virtue Theories

Mid-twentieth century se kai writers ne aisi theories banaane ki koshish ki hain jo Kantian aur consequentialist approaches ki kuch perceived objectionable features se bachein, aur aisa karte hue unhone un cheezon ka appeal kiya hai jo wo essentially Aristotelian themes maante hain. Is task ko approach karne ke do tareeke hain, dono hi best kism ki human life ke notion ko prominent banate hain. Ek approach ye hai ki is idea ko chhod diya jaaye ki ek ethical theory ko right action ka apna khud ka criterion dena hi hoga. Isse iske bajaye is sawaal par zor dena chahiye ki insaan ko kaisa person hona chahiye, aur kaise jeena chahiye, human nature aur shayad us community ki nature ko bhi dhyan mein rakhte hue jisme insaan rehta hai. Doosra tareeka ek aisi theory develop karna hai jo (pehle wale ki tarah) human flourishing aur virtues ko central role deti hai, lekin jo right action ka bhi ek criterion offer karne ka daava karti hai, jo upar discuss ki gayi dono rivals se bahut different kism ka hai.

Is doosre version ki 'Virtue Ethics' ke central tenets ye hain: (1) Ek virtuous action ko virtuous banane wali cheez ye hai ki ye wo hai jo ek virtuous agent relevant circumstances mein karega; (2) Ek virtuous agent wo hai jiske paas virtues hain; (3) Virtues wo character-traits hain jo ek insaan ko flourish karne dete hain, yani best life jeene dete hain. Ab (2) aur (3) ka Aristotelian provenance (mool) clear hai, aur (3) ek highly controversial claim hai, jahan tak ye assume karti hai ki virtues zaroor unhe possess karne wale ko benefit karti hain. Aisa nahi ki wo aise benefit karti hain jaise medicine ek sick person ko karti hai — kyunki healthy hona medicine ke bina bhi attain kiya ja sakta hai, jabki, question mein di gayi theory ke according, virtues ke bina flourishing (eudaimonia) possible hi nahi hai; ye possessor ke liye intrinsically, na ki instrumentally, beneficial hain. Lekin kya humein Aristotle ko (1) ka bhi credit dena chahiye?

'Haan' jawaab II.4 mein ek remark se suggest ho sakta hai: 'Actions, tab, just aur temperate kehlaate hain jab wo aisi hoti hain jaisi just ya temperate aadmi karega.' Lekin arguably is remark ka point kuch aur hai, jaisa context indicate karta hai. Is chapter ka purpose Aristotle ke us claim ko establish karna hai ki aap ek just act kar sakte hain bina abhi just person hue; unhe iski zaroorat hai apne is important view ke liye ki hum just acts karke hi just bante hain. Upar wale quotation mein wo shayad simply point ko drive home kar rahe hon, jaisa ki us quotation ke sequel se suggest hota hai. Agar wo (1) ke prati committed nahi hain, aur agar ye modern Virtue Ethics ke liye ek key tenet maana jaata hai — so-called virtues ki explanatory priority right ya virtuous actions ke upar — to us extent tak modern Virtue Ethics Aristotle ke intended se aage badh jaati hai.

Lekin agar hum deny karein ki Aristotle ne (1) hold kiya, to sawaal phir uthta hai: Aristotle ke liye, kisi diye gaye action ko just ya temperate action kya banata hai? Humne pehle hi dekha hai ki unki moral virtues ki theory is idea ko essentially appeal karti hai ki ek virtuous action wo hai jahan agent kisi given situation mein appropriate ('intermediate' ya 'mean') feel aur karta hai. Lekin, jaisa Aristotle khud admit karte hain (VI. 1), ye kisi aise insaan se zyada helpful nahi hai jo healthy hona chahta hai use ye kehna 'wahi karo jo medical science prescribe karti hai'. Aristotle ka ek aur important claim ye hai ki ek virtuous person kuch actions 'noble (kalon) ki wajah se' choose karta hai. Ye bhi baat clear nahi karta. 'Noble ke liye' act karna kya hai? Kya ek agent ko is thought se act karna hoga ki uska action noble hai? (Shayad nahi, lekin kam se kam 'pride' ki virtue wala aadmi to aisa karega, IV.3.) Aur act karne ke kaunse characteristics ise noble qualify karte hain? Humare text mein in sawaalon ke koi clear jawaab nahi hain, aur 'noble' ki koi systematic discussion nahi hai.

Lekin Aristotle, main sochta hoon, right action ke criterion ki demand ko outright reject kar denge. Wo expect karte hain ki ek well-brought-up person ko kaafi achha idea ho ki kisi bhi given situation mein achhe se act karne ke liye kaunse features aur considerations relevant hain, aur ye considerations kai aur various kism ke honge. Action ke kya consequences hain, khaas kar common good ke liye, ek factor hoga; jo relationship agent ka involved doosri parties ke saath hai, wo bhi crucial hoga. So-called self-regarding virtues ke liye, jaise temperance, wo ye obvious maanenge ki kuch kism ka indulgence ek good human life ke liye appropriate hi nahi hai, na sirf isliye ki ye kisi ki activities ko impair karta hai, balki apne aap mein bhi. Kuch cases mein, khaas kar justice mein, wo isse zyada bata paate hain ki ek action ko just kya banata hai, lekin us respect mein, jaisa wo khud admit karte hain (V. 5), justice doosri virtues se kuch different hai. Hum justice aur friendship par neeche wapas aayenge, Aristotle ke ethical theorizing ke method ki discussion ke baad.

## Aristotle's Method and Meta-ethical Assumptions

### Method

Work ki shuruaat mein hi (1.3) Aristotle apne method ke baare mein thoda kehte hain: wo un many opinions ko examine karenge jo sabse prevalent (prachalit) hain, aur arguable, yani plausible hain. Incontinence aur related 'affections' (VII.1) ki apni discussion introduce karne wale ek famous passage mein wo likhte hain:

*"Humein, jaise doosre sabhi cases mein, apparent facts humare saamne rakhne chahiye aur, pehle difficulties discuss karke, aage badh kar prove karna chahiye, agar possible ho, in affections of the mind ke baare mein sabhi common opinions ki truth, ya, agar ye nahi ho paaye, to zyadatar aur sabse authoritative opinions ki; kyunki agar hum difficulties resolve bhi karein aur common opinions ko undisturbed bhi chhode, to hum case ko sufficiently prove kar chuke honge."*

Lekin unke method ki ye important feature — iska dialectical (bahas-aadharit) character — bahut saare sawaal uthata hai. Humein pata hai ki wo common opinions ko explain karne ki koshish karte hain aur ye ki wo kyun clash karte hain, agar karte hain to. Lekin iss se kaisi theory milti hai? Kya ye sirf common sense morality hai jise regiment aur consistent banaya gaya hai? Kya everyday moral opinions ko carefully dekhna unhe undermine nahi kar sakta — jaise Plato ke kuch main speakers, jaise Thrasymachus, ke immoralist critiques ke manner mein? Aristotle kabhi bhi apni dialectical inquiry ke outcome ke roop mein aisa hone ka envisage nahi karte lagte. Kisi bhi surat mein, unke paas sirf common opinions se zyada tools hain, halaanki ye important zaroor hain. Wo apni doosri philosophical works se bhi kuch theses draw karenge. Humne pehle hi human nature aur human function (1.7) ke unke appeal ki discussion ki hai. Prominent bhi ye thesis hai ki activity ya actuality, potentiality ya capacity se superior hai: isi se ye explain hota hai ki happiness (ek activity) virtue (ek state, yani ek tarah ki potentiality) kyun nahi ho sakti. Soul ke parts (1.13 aur VI. 1) aur gods ki nature (X.7 aur 8) ke baare mein views bhi important hain.

### Meta-ethical Assumptions

Common opinions ko itna weight dene ke bawajood, Aristotle bilkul bhi ethics ke ek relativist ya subjectivist view ko adopt karne ke liye tempted nahi hain. Yani, wo na to ye maante hain ki right aur wrong sirf kisi diye gaye society ke relative ho sakte hain, na hi ye ki right aur wrong simply is baat ka matter hai ki koi ya koi group kya believe karta hai.

Unhone aise views encounter kiye the, aur inhe early on refer karte hain (I.3):

*"Ab noble aur just actions, jinhe political science investigate karti hai, itni variety aur fluctuation exhibit karti hain, ki inhe sirf convention se exist karne wali maana ja sakta hai, nature se nahi."*

Kuch thinkers ne sach mein ye conclusion nikala ki just aur noble (kalon) sirf convention se exist karte hain. Variety aur fluctuation jiski wo baat kar rahe hain wo do tarah ki hai: just ke baare mein views ki variety, aur ye fact ki just circumstance ke hisaab se fluctuate karta hai. Ye facts explain karte hain ki kyun kuch logo ne conclude kiya ki saari morality convention (nomos) ka matter hai, yani, logo ke believe karne ka, ya jo law mein enshrine kiya gaya hai, uska matter hai, aur ki 'nature se' kuch bhi right ya wrong nahi hai. V.7 mein wo is subject par wapas aate hain, is baar ye discuss karte hue ki kya justice simply nomos, law ya convention, ka matter hai, ya kya kuch cheezein nature se just ya unjust hain. Phir wo remark karte hain ki jo view ye suggest karta hai ki saari justice convention ka matter hai, wo justice ki variability ki recognition se prompt hota hai.

Response mein, Aristotle ek aisa position adopt karte hain jiske kaafi merit hain, aur ek mistake expose karte hain jo aaj bhi modern ethics discussions mein commonly paayi jaati hai. Modern terms mein rakhein, to wo moral objectivity insist karte hain, jabki universality deny karte hain. Jo view wo oppose karte hain wo sochta hai ki, universally true moral judgements ke bina, koi objectively true moral judgements nahi ho sakte. Aur ye, wo rightly point out karte hain, ek error hai. Right ya wrong, just ya unjust, ke baare mein judgements objectively true ya false ho sakte hain; unki truth sirf kisi ke unhe true believe karne se zyada kuch hai. Ye follow nahi hota, wo insist karte hain, ki ethical truths ka form 'debt ka payment withhold karna hamesha unjust hai' ya 'aapko hamesha apne father ki authority ko defer karna chahiye' jaisa hona chahiye. Chahe ye usually correct ho, exceptions ho sakte hain, jinhe phronimos, trained moral understanding wala insaan, recognize karega. Aapko ye insist karne ki zaroorat nahi hai ki X-karna hamesha ya universally wrong hai, ye maanne ke liye ki is occasion par X-karna wrong hoga, ye objectively true hai. Ye point unke equity (V.10) ki discussion mein develop kiya gaya hai. Is virtue mein ye recognize karna shaamil hai ki legal justice ko particular case ke suit karne ke liye kahan modify karne ki zaroorat hai.

In objective moral truths ka basis kya hai, ye ek difficult matter hai, aur, jaisa humne upar dekha, Aristotle iska koi clear jawaab nahi dete. Isme wo — arguably — kisi bhi doosre moral philosopher se zyada bura nahi karte. Lekin abhi describe ki gayi mistake ko nail karna (pakadna) ek signal achievement thi.

### Other-Regarding and Self-Regarding Concerns

Jaisa humne dekha hai, Aristotle maante hain ki apni khud ki happiness ko us end ke roop mein banana rational hai jisse main aim karta hoon. Unki ethics is baat ka study hai ki happiness kya hai aur ise kaise achieve kiya jaaye, aur virtues ke unke account mein assume hota hai ki virtues — moral aur intellectual dono — essentially, aisi states hain jo apne possessor ko benefit karti hain. Kya isse morality ka point miss nahi hota, kuch poochenge? Wo dusron ke liye concern ke liye kya room chhodte hain, us zaroorat ke liye ki wo humpar unke claims ko recognize karein, aur, kabhi-kabhi, unhe khud se aur apni khud ki happiness se priority dein?

In sawaalon ka poora jawaab is brief introduction mein nahi diya ja sakta, lekin do ab tak undiscussed topics ko consider karne se help milegi: justice aur friendship.

### Justice (Book V)

'Justice doosre ka good hai' Thrasymachus ne declare kiya (Plato, Republic 1), aur isliye wo isse have karne layak nahi declare karte hain. Aristotle pehli baat se agree karte hain, lekin phir bhi ise ek virtue maante hain. Ek virtue ke roop mein ye agent ke liye bhi good honi chahiye jitni doosron ke liye, halaanki Aristotle ye spell out nahi karte ki ye kaise hai. Iske bajaye, wo kuch important distinctions draw karte hain, jinme se kuch ne tab se justice ke accounts ko dominate kiya hai. Pehle wo 'universal' justice aur injustice ko 'particular' justice aur injustice se distinguish karte hain. Universal justice, wo kehte hain, virtue ka poora hissa hai uske other-regarding aspect mein. Particular justice ek specific kism ki virtue hai, jiski aage important subdivisions hain.

'Virtue ka poora hissa uske other-regarding aspect mein' ek interesting concept hai, ye suggest karta hai ki kisi tareeke se moral virtue ki saari branches, unhe bhi jinhe hum self-regarding maante hain jaise temperance, dusron ke saath humare relations involve karti hain. Lekin Aristotle is intriguing idea ko aage develop nahi karte. Iske bajaye wo 'particular justice' mein jaate hain, distributive justice — wo variety jo goods aur burdens ke fair distribution se deal karti hai — ko rectificatory justice se distinguish karte hue. Pehle mein wo ye crucial observation karte hain ki goods ko fairly share karne ka matlab involved parties ke relevant merits ko dhyan mein rakhna hai, aur un merits ke proportion mein goods ko share karna hai. Lekin wo ye discuss karne ki koshish nahi karte ki dhyan mein rakhne ka right basis kya hai. Aisa karke wo fair distribution ka ek formal lekin substantive nahi account dete hain. Doosre ke liye — rectificatory justice — just punishment discuss karne ke bajaye, jaisa hum expect kar sakte the, wo victim ke liye cheezein sahi karne par focus karte hain — isi se 'rectificatory' label aata hai. In chapters mein (kuch mathematical illustrations ki 'help' se) jo conceptual distinctions wo draw karte hain, wo important hain. Lekin hum vain mein justly act karne ki defence dhoondte hain, jo sceptic ki us worry ka jawaab de sake ki justly act karna doosron ko benefit karta hai, khud ko nahi.

### Friendship

Poori work ka ek-paanchwa hissa (Books VIII aur IX) friendship ko diya gaya hai, jo Aristotle ke liye iski importance ka nishaan hai. In books mein humein ethics mein other-regarding concerns ke role ki further exploration milti hai. Lekin ye un moralities se bahut different shape leta hai jo impartiality par zor deti hain. Unke account mein, jinhe hum apna friend consider kar sakte hain wo fellow citizens tak bhi extend ho sakte hain lekin agent se kuch relation crucial hai. ('Friends' term mein loved ones, jaise relatives, business associates, aur doosre bhi shaamil hain.) Ye idea ki humari dusron ke prati simply as such obligations ho sakti hain, Aristotle se, aur un sabhi moralities se jinse wo familiar the, completely absent hai. Best kism ki friendship, wo maintain karte hain, un logo ke saath friendship hai jinke liye hum well-wish karte hain aur jinke saath hum shared valuable activities mein time spend kar sakte hain, sab kuch unki virtue ki wajah se. Pleasure aur utility par based friendships bhi exist karti hain, wo allow karte hain, lekin sirf pehli kism perfect friendship hai.

Ek taraf hum is restriction se puzzled ho sakte hain, aur protest kar sakte hain ki true friendship un logo ke beech bhi exist kar sakti hai jo virtuous people nahi hain. Doosri taraf, humein lag sakta hai ki Aristotle abhi bhi ek zyada egocentric approach rakhte hain jab wo argue karte hain ki friends ke saath hamara relation kisi tarah khud ke saath hamare relation se derivative hai (IX.3). Self-love (IX.8) par ek important chapter mein humein wo ye recognize karte milte hain, aur poori tarah resolve nahi karte, apne egocentric approach aur apne is correct insistence ke beech ki tension ki friendship mein insaan doosre ke good ko us doosre ki khaatir wish karta hai.

Friendship wali books mein, Aristotle ki writings ke contrasting aspects bahut evident hain. Unki zyada theoretical discussions obscure aur strained lag sakti hain, jaise IX.9 mein unka convoluted proof jo dikhata hai ki happy person ko virtuous friends ki zaroorat kyun hai. Lekin bahut se points par, jaise chapters (VIII.13–IX.3) mein jo specific issues discuss karte hain — friendship ki casuistry — aur unki mothers ke apne bacchon ke liye pyaar ke baare mein insightful remarks mein, humein Aristotle friendship ki nature aur value ki ek sure touch aur zyada plausible grasp dikhaate milte hain.

## Aristotle's Moral Psychology

### Voluntary and Involuntary

Virtue ke students aur legislators, dono ko voluntariness ke basis ke baare mein jaanna zaroori hai (III. 1). Aristotle ne jo main outlines diye hain wo time ke test mein khare utre hain. Knowledge ki kami aur power ki kami (yani 'forced' hona), aaj ke law mein chief excusing conditions, wo criteria hain jo unhone kisi act ke voluntary na hone ke liye identify kiye. Wo force proper aur un cases mein important distinction draw karte hain jahan log claim karte hain ki wo compelled the, lekin jahan external force involved nahi hai. Ye doosre wale hain 'wo cheezein jo bade evils ke fear se ya kisi noble object ke liye ki jaati hain (jaise agar ek tyrant kisi ko kuch base karne ka order de, uske parents aur bacchon ko apne power mein rakhte hue, aur agar wo action karne se wo bach jaate lekin otherwise maar diye jaate)': kyunki insaan ne ye is wajah se kiya ki kuch aur bura na ho, ye actions na to forced hain, na hi involuntary. Inhe 'mixed, lekin voluntary jaisi zyada' label karte hue wo ek vital point add karte hain: chahe ye aapka act ho, aur isliye involuntary nahi, iska matlab ye nahi ki aapko ise karne ke liye blame kiya jaana chahiye. Praise (agar aapne cargo jettison (fenk) karke crew bachaya) ya pardon (agar aapne terrible pressure mein ek wrongful act kiya) appropriate ho sakta hai.

Ye discussion ki ignorance kab kisi act ko involuntary banati hai, complex hai, lekin isme ek important distinction hai. Excuse 'mujhe nahi pata tha' exculpate (dosh-mukt) karne ke liye sufficient nahi hai; jo matter karta hai wo ye hai ki ignorance aapki galti thi ya nahi (III.5). Isliye drunkenness ya anger ki wajah se ignorance mein kiye gaye acts involuntary nahi maane jaate, aur blame ke liye liable hain. III.5 mein Aristotle (sceptics ke against) ye dikhane ki koshish karte hain ki hum apne good aur bad characters ke liye bhi utne hi responsible hain jitne apne good aur bad actions ke liye. Ye moral responsibility ke basis ke modern problem ki discussion ke sabse close hai jo wo aate hain. Wo sceptics ke arguments ko strongly rakhte hain, aur, halaanki wo unhe dismiss karte hain, shayad apne conclusion formulate karte waqt unhe kuch concede (maan) karte hain: 'hum apne states of character ke somehow part-causes hain'.

### Choice

Ek law-court mein, aur praise ya blame ke liye, jo matter karta hai wo ye hai ki act voluntary tha ya nahi. Lekin virtue aur vice ke sawaalon ke liye jo matter karta hai wo ye hai ki aapke acts sirf voluntary hi nahi balki chosen bhi hain ya nahi. Choice, prohairesis, ek important lekin puzzling concept bhi hai. Ye humari everyday choice ki notion se narrower hai (aur sach mein Aristotle ke paas ek concept hai jo usse zyada close hai, hairesis). Ek bachcha choice manifest nahi kar sakta (is technical sense mein), aur jab main anger mein, ya incontinent tareeke se, act karta hoon, mere acts voluntary hote hain — isliye praise ya blame kiye ja sakte hain — lekin chosen nahi hote. Sirf ek adult jiske paas ek settled aur reasoned state of character hai, Aristotle ke sense mein choices bana sakta hai. Wo choice ki discussion karte hain (III.2-3), aur conclude karte hain ki ye 'humari power mein cheezon ka deliberate desire' hai. Virtue ko (II.6) ek state of character ke roop mein define kiya gaya jo choice se concerned hai, doosre shabdon mein, choices mein issue hota hai, aur vice bhi same hai (siwaye is ke ki ek ke choices good hote hain aur doosre ke bad). To, jaisa Aristotle admit karte hain (V.9), unjust hona kaafi mushkil hai! Bahut saare log virtuous acts karte hain bina abhi virtuous hue — jaise wo sab jo abhi virtuous hona seekh rahe hain (II. 1, 4). Unke acts praise kiye ja sakte hain, halaanki unhe abhi ek virtue possess karne ka credit nahi diya ja sakta. Aur bahut saare log bad acts karte hain bina vicious hue; unke bad acts voluntary hain, aur blame deserve karte hain, lekin choices nahi hain. Isके signal examples incontinent actions hain.

### Incontinence

Kya koi ye jaan sakta hai ki best kya hai, lekin us knowledge ke against act kar sakta hai, pleasure ya pain ya anger ya kisi aisi passion se overcome ho kar? Haan, kehte hain 'the many', yani zyadatar log; nahi, counter karte hain Socrates Plato ki dialogue Protagoras mein. Socrates ki theses ki virtue knowledge hai aur ki jo person best jaanta hai wo hamesha wahi karega, wo cheezein hain jinhe Aristotle kai jagah touch karte hain (VI.13, VII.1–3). Pehle hi II.4 mein wo argue kar chuke hain ki, virtue possess karne ke liye, jaanna itna important nahi tha jitna reliably choose karna, apne aap ke liye, virtuous actions. To hum expect karte hain ki wo Socrates ke against 'many' ke saath side lenge, aur common opinion accept karenge ki 'incontinent aadmi, ye jaante hue ki jo wo kar raha hai bad hai, passion ke result mein wahi karta hai' (VII.I). Wo certainly affirm karte hain ki incontinent actions voluntary aur blameworthy hain. Lekin robustly insist karne ke bajaye ki incontinent person poori knowledge mein act karta hai ki jo wo kar raha hai bad hai, wo different tareeko se jaanne ke, aur practical reasoning ke different parts (premisses) ke beech distinctions ka ek set develop karte hain. Ek point par — seemingly Socrates ke saath side lete hue — wo ek incontinent person ko un logo se compare karte hain jo asleep, mad, ya drunk hain. Ye seemingly is wajah se hai kyunki, strong passion ke influence mein — jaise ek aur drink ka desire — incontinent person ka knowledge (jaise, ki ye ek zyada hai) temporarily inaccessible ho jaata hai. Kya Aristotle kisi bhi point par ('the many' ke saath) allow karte hain ki incontinently act karte waqt kisi person ka knowledge aur reasoning unimpaired ho sakta hai? Is difficult chapter (VII.3) ke bahut diverse interpretations hain, aur different answers hain. Ek cheez clear hai: Aristotle apne aap ko us kisi bhi view (Plato ya Socrates ke) se door rakhna chahte hain jo saari vice ko incontinence ya self-control ki kami se equate karti hai. Vicious person ke ulat, incontinent ke paas right overall standards aur choices hain, aur, kam se kam is extent tak, ye jaanta hai ki jo wo abhi kar raha hai bad hai, chahe, kisi doosre tareeke se, act karne ke exact moment par, wo poori knowledge mein aisa nahi karta.

## Concluding Remarks

Kya ethics mein kaam ka relevance aur sach hona bhi sabhi historical periods mein ho sakta hai? Kuch writers ek broadly Aristotelian tradition mein — jaise A. MacIntyre — aisi ambition ko disown karte hain, ye maante hue ki best life ki koi bhi conception aur virtues kya hain, ye necessarily ek diye gaye historical period aur community mein grounded hoti hai. Aristotle aise koi qualms nahi dikhaate; wo ek aisi theory present karne mein concerned lagte hain jo apne scope mein zyada universal hai, human nature ke ek account par based, as such. Lekin, do aur half millennia baad aur morality aur moral theory mein developments ke light mein, aaj ke readers unke kuch assumptions aur values se struck, aur even appalled (chaunk jaana), ho sakte hain. Aristotle slavery, aur women ke liye ek lowly status, ko bina sawaal ke accept karte hain, halaanki apne bacchon ke liye women ke pyaar par unki remarks telling aur sympathetic hain. Translation 'man' ke frequent use ko avoid karne ki koshish nahi karta — jaise 'the truthful man', 'the boastful man', 'the good-tempered man', waghera mein. Kyunki jo virtues aur vices describe kiye gaye hain wo males ke hain, aur unki shuru se hi assumption ye hai ki best life sirf well-born, well-educated male citizens hi jee sakte hain jinhe apni khud ki living kamaane ki zaroorat nahi. Sirf yehi log un virtues (intellectual aur/ya moral) ko develop kar sakte hain jinki practice mein happiness ki life consist karti hai.

Aur virtues aur vices ki list mein humein kuch surprising inclusions milte hain, aur waise hi omissions bhi. Humne pehle hi dekha hai ki impartiality Aristotle ke liye koi ideal nahi thi, halaanki aaj ise kis extent tak ek value maana jaana chahiye, ye ek controversial matter hai. Waise hi ye idea ki humein suffering ko alleviate karne aur apne fellow men ki help karne ki koshish karni chahiye chahe wo humse kitne bhi remote hon, Aristotle ke liye, aur sach mein unke time ke sabhi logo ke liye, kaafi foreign hai. Na to kindness na hi cruelty ka mention milta hai, us proximity ke bawajood jo Aristotle ki ruthless conqueror Alexander se thi. Aristotle ke liye truthfulness ki virtue apni merits ke baare mein appropriately open hone ka matter hai, general truth-telling ka nahi. Do grand-scale virtues shayad pehli nazar mein sabse foreign lagti hain: magnificence — large-scale public donations appropriately karna (IV.2), aur megalopsuchia, literally 'greatness of soul' lekin yahan 'pride' translate ki gayi hai (IV.3). Properly proud aadmi great honour ke worthy hai aur khud ko aisa jaanta hai. Wo appropriately act karta hai; wo few deeds ka aadmi hai lekin great aur noble wale, ek slow step ke saath, ek deep voice, aur ek level utterance ke saath. Lekin yahan bhi modern readers ko kuch admirable features milte hain: proud aadmi apne saath kiye gaye wrongs ko overlook karta hai; apna mind freely bolta hai; equals ke beech apni dignity maintain karta hai lekin lower social status wale logo ke saath unassuming (bina dikhawe ke) hai; gossip nahi karta ya grudges nahi rakhta.

Bahut se readers ke liye Aristotle ki ethics ka sabse alien aspect shayad iska (X. 7, 8) philosophical contemplation ki life ko kisi bhi doosri life se upar, active citizen ki life sameit jo moral virtues develop aur manifest karta hai, ultimate elevation ho. Humne pehle hi work ke end mein is shayad surprising development par scholarly dispute note ki hai. Phir bhi, bahut saari traditions, Western aur Eastern dono, us life ko highest place dete hain jisme insaan apni zyada human nature aur human virtues ki zaroorat se transcend (paar) karta hai. Ek different perspective se, humein surprise ho sakta hai ki Aristotle kabhi bhi, best life ke ek candidate ke roop mein, poet ya dramatist, artist ya sculptor ki life consider nahi karte. Iska reason, main sochta hoon, ek metaphysical assumption mein milta hai: koi bhi activity jiska khud se aage ek end ho, isi wajah se un activities se kam valuable hai jo khud mein ends hain. Ek hi jhatke mein creativity ke saare forms demote (neeche) ho jaate hain, shaayad Aristotle ki apni productive investigations bhi jo sabhi philosophical matters mein hain. Phir bhi, unke is strange claim ke bawajood ki jo log jaante hain wo apna time zyada pleasantly bitaate hain un logo se jo inquire karte hain (X.7), hum shayad Aristotle ki inquiry ki life ke liye thankful ho sakte hain.

# NOTE ON THE TEXT AND TRANSLATION

Ye translation noted (mashhoor) Aristotle scholar, W. D. Ross, ki hai. Ye pehli baar 1925 mein The Works of Aristotle translated into English (Oxford University Press) ke volume ix mein publish hui thi. Ye Nicomachean Ethics ke Oxford Classical Text (ed. Bywater) par based hai, jisse, Ross ne likha tha, wo sirf occasionally hate the, jahan aisa lagta tha ki aisa karne se kaafi kuch gain ho sakta hai. Ye translation 1973 mein J. O. Urmson dwara revise ki gayi thi, aur 1980 mein World's Classics mein J. L. Ackrill ki further revisions ke saath publish hui.

Is present edition ke liye bahut kam aur changes kiye gaye hain. Pehle ke revisers ki tarah, current editor ne Ross ki central terminology ki translation ko unchanged chhoda hai, kuch bahut kam exceptions ke saath. Jaise, logos, jise Ross usually 'rational principle' ya 'rule' translate karte the, maine (editor) isse almost hamesha 'reason' se render kiya hai; aur maine orthos logos ko 'correct reason' translate kiya hai. III. 1 mein 'force' aur uske cognates ab consistently bia ke liye use kiye jaate hain, 'compelled'/'compulsion' anagkē ke cognates ke liye. Maine 'for the sake of the noble' substitute kiya hai jahan Ross aksar 'for honour's sake' rakhte the jab Greek mein kalon use hota tha. V. 5 mein literal translation 'need' ab chreia ke liye 'demand' ki jagah leta hai. Kuch jagahon par ek ab almost obsolete (purana pad chuka) term replace kiya gaya hai (jaise, 'draught' ki jagah 'drink', 'unguent' ki jagah 'perfume'). Assi saal se zyada pehle likhi gayi, ye translation aaj bhi justly admired (sahi maayno mein sarahi jaane wali) hai, aur isse bahut kam further adjustment ki zaroorat padi hai.

Numbering system, jaise 1098b10 jaise references, wahi hai jo sabhi modern editors, translators, aur commentators use karte hain. Ye numbers (Bekker numbers) Aristotle ke works ke 1831 ke Berlin edition se aaye hain. Marginal numbers Greek text ke corresponding hote hain; iska matlab hai ki translation mein kabhi-kabhi correspondence exact nahi hoti. Secondary literature use karne walon ke liye, Bekker line numbers ka hona phir bhi ek invaluable help hai. Jahan translation mein koi passage square brackets mein enclosed hai, us jagah ke corresponding Greek words ko Oxford Classical Text ke editor dwara ek marginal gloss (haashiye ki tippani) maana jaata hai.

Reference II.4, for example, Book II chapter 4 ko refer karta hai. Books mein division antiquity (puraane zamaane) tak jaata hai, jabki chapters mein division zyada recent hai. Kabhi-kabhi chapter breaks ek illogical (bemaani) point par aate hain.

Jo headings aur summaries use kiye gaye hain, saath hi translation ki body mein kabhi-kabhi aane wale numbers, (kuch jo maine revise kiye hain unhe chhod kar) wahi hain jo Ross ne introduce kiye the. Footnoted cross-references bhi Ross ki original translation mein supplied thi. Introduction ki tarah, explanatory endnotes is edition ke liye naye likhe gaye hain. Ye pehle wale notes se kaafi zyada fuller (bhare-pure) hain, lekin bahut hi occasionally maine verbatim (jaise ka taisa), acknowledgement ke saath, Ross ka original note quote kiya hai. Endnotes ko text mein ek asterisk se indicate kiya gaya hai.

Main J. L. Ackrill ki writings (aur lectures) ke prati, aur S. Broadie aur C. Rowe, aur T. H. Irwin ke kaam ke prati, unke respective translations aur notes mein, apna debt (rin/ehsaan) acknowledge karna chahunga, saath hi Books II–IV par C. C. W. Taylor ki commentary ke prati bhi.

Nicomachean Ethics antiquity se lekar aaj tak innumerable (anginat) commentaries ka subject rahi hai, khaas kar St Thomas Aquinas dwara; ek estimate ke according, Middle Ages se lekar aaj tak har decade mein lagbhag ek commentary aayi hai. Jo bhi is work par comment karne ki himmat karte hain, wo apne predecessors (pehle wale scholars) ki mehnat ke profound debt (gehra ehsaan) mein hain.
